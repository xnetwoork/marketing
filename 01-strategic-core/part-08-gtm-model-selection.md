# Go-To-Market Model Selection — PLG, SLG & Hybrid

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Understand the mechanics and requirements of Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- Apply the Sales-Led Growth (SLG) model in the right contexts
- Design a hybrid GTM motion that combines PLG and SLG intelligently
- Select the right GTM model based on your product, market, and stage
- Align your customer acquisition motion with your chosen GTM type
- Know how and when to switch models
- Track the right metrics for each GTM approach

---

## What Is a GTM Model?

Your **Go-To-Market (GTM) model** is the fundamental mechanism by which your company acquires, activates, and expands customers. It determines who drives growth (product, sales, or both), how buyers experience your product before purchase, and where revenue actually comes from.

Choosing the wrong GTM model doesn't just slow growth — it misallocates resources, demoralizes teams, and creates structural dysfunction that's expensive to undo.

---

## Product-Led Growth (PLG)

### What It Is

In a PLG model, **the product itself is the primary acquisition, conversion, and expansion mechanism.** Users discover and adopt the product before a sales conversation ever happens — often without one at all.

### PLG Mechanics

**The PLG flywheel:**
1. **Acquisition** — User signs up via free trial or freemium (often virally through referral or word-of-mouth)
2. **Activation** — User reaches the "aha moment" — the specific action that delivers first value
3. **Retention** — Habitual usage creates dependency and switching cost
4. **Expansion** — Usage growth triggers upgrade (feature gates, seat limits, volume limits)
5. **Referral** — Satisfied users invite colleagues; viral loop closes

### PLG Requirements

PLG only works if:
- ✅ The product can demonstrate value **without a sales rep explaining it**
- ✅ Time-to-value is **short** — ideally under 10 minutes for the first "aha"
- ✅ The problem is **broad and self-serve-friendly** (not requiring heavy customization)
- ✅ The buyer and user are the **same person** (or closely related)
- ✅ Viral or bottom-up loops exist (individuals adopt, then teams follow)

### PLG Examples

| Company | PLG Motion | Viral loop |
|---|---|---|
| Slack | Free team messaging → team adoption → seat expansion | Invite coworkers |
| Figma | Free design tool → shared files → team adoption | Share design files |
| Notion | Free workspace → template sharing → team onboarding | Public pages, templates |
| Calendly | Free scheduling → recipient sees product in every invite | Link sharing |

### PLG Metrics

| Metric | Target benchmark |
|---|---|
| Time-to-activate (first value moment) | < 10 minutes |
| Free-to-paid conversion rate | 3–15% |
| Product-Qualified Lead (PQL) rate | 10–30% of free users |
| Viral coefficient (K-factor) | > 0.5 is strong; > 1.0 is compounding |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | > 110% indicates healthy expansion |

## Sales-Led Growth (SLG)

### What It Is

In an SLG model, **human sellers drive acquisition, conversion, and expansion.** The product may or may not be accessible before a deal closes; the rep is the primary interface between prospect and company.

### SLG Mechanics

**The SLG funnel:**
1. **Awareness** — Marketing generates pipeline (content, events, paid, outbound)
2. **Prospecting** — SDRs identify and qualify target accounts
3. **Discovery** — AE runs discovery to confirm pain, fit, and urgency
4. **Demo/Proposal** — Tailored demo or proposal converts interest to evaluation
5. **Close** — Negotiation, procurement, legal → signed contract
6. **Onboarding** — CS manages implementation and activation
7. **Expansion** — AE or CS manages upsell and renewal

### SLG Requirements

SLG works best when:
- ✅ The product requires customization, integration, or configuration to deliver value
- ✅ Deal size justifies a human sales motion (typically ACV > $5,000)
- ✅ Buyers are risk-averse and need reassurance (compliance, enterprise security)
- ✅ Buying committee is large — multiple stakeholders must align
- ✅ The problem is strategic or complex — not self-evident from a free trial

### SLG Examples

Salesforce, Workday, ServiceNow, Veeva — all enterprise software with high ACV, complex implementation, and multi-stakeholder deals.

### SLG Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Sales cycle length | 30–180 days depending on ACV |
| Win rate (qualified pipeline) | 20–35% |
| CAC Payback Period | < 18 months |
| Pipeline coverage ratio | 3–4× quota |
| AE quota attainment | > 65% of reps at quota |

## Hybrid PLG + SLG

### What It Is

The hybrid model uses PLG for **top-of-funnel expansion and bottom-up adoption**, while layering SLG on top to **accelerate enterprise deals and unlock larger contracts.**

### How It Works

**PLG as the top of funnel:**
- Product generates free signups and usage data
- Usage patterns surface Product-Qualified Accounts (PQAs) — companies with organic adoption
- Sales targets PQAs with high intent data instead of cold outbound

**SLG as the enterprise motion:**
- Sales engages accounts showing expansion signals or enterprise fit
- Converts departmental PLG usage into company-wide enterprise contracts
- Handles negotiation, compliance, procurement, and expansion

**The "land and expand" hybrid pattern:**
1. Individual user discovers product (PLG)
2. Team adopts via viral loop (PLG)
3. Usage hits a threshold that triggers sales outreach (PQA scoring)
4. AE converts team account to paid plan and expands to department (SLG)
5. Enterprise contract covers entire organization (SLG)

> **Examples:** Slack, Figma, Atlassian, Dropbox — all started as pure PLG and added enterprise SLG as they matured.

## When to Use Each Model

| Scenario | Best GTM Model |
|---|---|
| Product delivers value immediately; simple sign-up | PLG |
| ACV < $5K; buyer = user; self-serve friendly | PLG |
| ACV > $25K; multi-stakeholder buy; long implementation | SLG |
| Complex regulatory or security requirements | SLG |
| Bottoms-up adoption pattern is emerging + enterprise deals needed | Hybrid |
| Existing PLG user base + need to unlock enterprise budgets | Hybrid |
| New market; need to educate buyers who don't know the problem | SLG |
| Viral product with network effects | PLG |

## Switching Between Models

GTM model switches are expensive and disruptive. Do them intentionally.

### PLG → Hybrid (most common evolution)
**Trigger:** PLG growth is strong but enterprise deals are being lost; large accounts are using product without a formal contract; NRR is good but new logo growth is plateauing.

**Execution:**
1. Build a PQL/PQA scoring model using product usage data
2. Hire an "enterprise overlay" AE team (not a traditional SLG sales team)
3. Build a sales-assist motion — AEs help PLG users convert, not replace PLG
4. Create enterprise tier with security, SSO, admin, and SLAs

### SLG → Hybrid
**Trigger:** Sales cycles are getting longer; competition from PLG-native companies is intensifying; SMB segment is being lost because acquisition cost is too high.

**Execution:**
1. Build a free tier or trial experience
2. Instrument product usage tracking
3. Create a self-serve buying path alongside enterprise sales
4. Measure which accounts activate self-serve vs. need sales touch

## GTM Motion Design

A GTM motion is the **repeatable sequence of actions that moves a prospect from awareness to revenue.**

**PLG motion design checklist:**
- [ ] Free tier or trial defined
- [ ] Onboarding flow drives to activation event in < 10 min
- [ ] In-product upgrade prompts at key moments
- [ ] Viral loop identified and instrumented
- [ ] PQL scoring model built and integrated with CRM

**SLG motion design checklist:**
- [ ] ICP defined and scored
- [ ] Outbound and inbound sourcing channels defined
- [ ] SDR → AE handoff criteria documented
- [ ] Discovery and demo playbooks built
- [ ] Win/loss tracking implemented

**Hybrid motion design checklist:**
- [ ] Both PLG and SLG motions above are functioning
- [ ] PQA scoring connects product data to sales queue
- [ ] Sales-assist vs. full-cycle AE roles defined
- [ ] Enterprise tier exists with appropriate packaging
- [ ] PLG and SLG metrics tracked separately and combined

## Metrics by GTM Type

| Metric | PLG | SLG | Hybrid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary acquisition metric | Signups / activations | SQLs generated | Signups + SQLs |
| Conversion metric | Free-to-paid % | Win rate % | PQL→SQL conversion |
| Revenue efficiency | CAC / Activation cost | CAC Payback (months) | Blended CAC by motion |
| Expansion metric | NRR / PQL → expansion | NRR / AE-driven upsell | NRR by acquisition source |
| Health metric | Activation rate, DAU | Quota attainment, pipeline coverage | Motion split %, overlap rate |

## Key Takeaways

- **GTM model is a strategic choice, not a default.** Choose it based on product characteristics, buyer behavior, and deal economics.
- **PLG requires a short time-to-value and a self-serve-friendly product** — not every product qualifies.
- **SLG scales with human capital** — it works, but it's expensive; ensure ACV justifies the cost.
- **Hybrid is the evolution path** for most successful PLG companies — PLG captures the market, SLG captures the revenue.
- **Track PLG and SLG metrics separately** — mixing them obscures which motion is performing and why.
- **Switch models proactively, not reactively** — waiting until growth stalls is the most expensive time to change.

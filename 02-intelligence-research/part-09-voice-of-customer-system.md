# Part 09: Voice-of-Customer (VoC) System

## Learning Objectives
- Build a systematic, repeatable VoC program that feeds directly into messaging
- Master customer interview techniques that surface real motivations
- Apply the Jobs-To-Be-Done framework to extract actionable insight
- Synthesize raw customer language into copy, positioning, and campaign strategy

---

## Why VoC Is a Competitive Moat

Most companies collect customer feedback. Few build a *system* around it. The difference is compounding: systematic VoC means your messaging, positioning, and product roadmap continuously converge on what customers actually value — while competitors guess.

**The core principle:** Your customers already know what your best ad copy should say. Your job is to listen, extract, and reflect it back.

---

## Building a Systematic VoC Program

A VoC system has four components:

| Component | Purpose | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **Collection** | Gather raw customer language | Ongoing |
| **Synthesis** | Group, tag, and interpret inputs | Weekly/Monthly |
| **Distribution** | Route insights to Marketing, Product, Sales | Monthly |
| **Governance** | Ensure quality, consistency, and action | Quarterly |

### Collection Channels
- **Customer interviews** (highest fidelity)
- **Support ticket mining** (real pain, real language)
- **Review site scraping** (G2, Trustpilot, Capterra, Amazon)
- **NPS/CSAT open-ends**
- **Sales call recordings** (Gong, Chorus)
- **Community and social listening**

## Customer Interview Techniques

### Who to Interview
Prioritize three segments:
1. **Recent converters** — Why did they buy now? What finally tipped them?
2. **Churned customers** — What failed to deliver?
3. **Long-term power users** — What do they love that others don't see?

### The Interview Structure (45–60 min)
1. **Context setting** (5 min) — Their role, company, day-to-day
2. **Before state** (10 min) — What was life like before your solution?
3. **Trigger moment** (10 min) — What caused them to look for a solution?
4. **Evaluation journey** (10 min) — How did they evaluate options?
5. **Post-purchase reality** (15 min) — What's better? What disappointed?
6. **Language harvesting** (10 min) — "How would you describe this to a peer?"

### Key Techniques
- **Use silence.** After an answer, wait 5 seconds. The best insight often comes in the discomfort.
- **Ask "what does that mean to you?"** Whenever they use abstract language ("easier," "faster," "better"), dig in.
- **Record and transcribe.** Never rely on notes alone. Tools: Otter.ai, Rev, Fireflies.

## Mining Reviews and Support Tickets

### Review Mining Protocol
1. Export reviews from G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, App Store
2. Tag each review: **Pain mentioned**, **Outcome achieved**, **Alternative compared**, **Objection raised**
3. Identify the top 5 phrases that appear across 3+ reviews verbatim — these are your headline candidates

### Support Ticket Analysis
Support tickets are a goldmine of *unfiltered* pain. Mine for:
- **Friction moments** — where do users get stuck?
- **Expectation gaps** — what did they think the product would do?
- **Language patterns** — how do they describe the problem they came to solve?

**Tool stack:** Zendesk exports + Dovetail or Airtable for tagging

## Jobs-To-Be-Done Applied to VoC

JTBD reframes the question from "what do customers want?" to "what are they *hiring* this product to do?"

### The Three Job Layers

| Job Type | Example | Marketing Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Functional** | "Send emails automatically" | Feature-led messaging |
| **Emotional** | "Feel confident my pipeline won't leak" | Anxiety/reassurance copy |
| **Social** | "Look like a data-driven marketer to my CEO" | Status and identity messaging |

### Applying JTBD to Interviews
Use the **Switch Interview** method:
- "Walk me through the moment you decided to change solutions."
- "What were you using before?"
- "What was the last straw?"
- "What did you hope switching would change?"

This surfaces the *trigger*, the *progress metric*, and the *job* in one conversation.

## Survey Design for Insight (Not Vanity)

Most VoC surveys measure satisfaction but not causation. Design for insight:

### High-Signal Survey Questions
- **PMF Question:** "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?" (Superhuman method)
- **Switching cost:** "What would you use if [product] didn't exist?"
- **Value clarity:** "What's the one thing [product] does that you couldn't live without?"
- **Referral language:** "How would you describe [product] to a colleague?"

### What to Avoid
- Leading questions ("How much do you love our new feature?")
- Rating scales without open-ends (numbers tell you *what*, not *why*)
- Too many questions (aim for under 5 for high completion)

## Synthesizing Insights Into Messaging

### The VoC Message Extraction Process

1. **Collect 50–100 data points** (interviews, reviews, tickets, surveys)
2. **Tag by theme:** Pain, Outcome, Objection, Comparator, Moment-of-truth
3. **Extract verbatim phrases** — these are your raw copy candidates
4. **Map to messaging pillars:** Which phrases speak to acquisition? Retention? Conversion?
5. **Test the top phrases** — run them in ads, subject lines, landing page headlines

> **Example:** If 12 customers independently say "I used to dread Monday morning pipeline reviews" — that's your headline. Not "Improve Sales Visibility."

## VoC Cadence and Governance

### Recommended Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Customer interviews | 4–6/month | PMM or Researcher |
| Review mining | Monthly | Marketing |
| Support ticket analysis | Monthly | CS + Marketing |
| VoC synthesis session | Monthly | Cross-functional |
| Messaging update review | Quarterly | PMM + Leadership |

### Governance Principles
- **Single source of truth:** Maintain a VoC repository (Notion, Airtable, Dovetail)
- **Attribution:** Tag every messaging change to a VoC insight
- **Closed-loop feedback:** If a VoC insight leads to a product change, document the outcome
- **Avoid HiPPO override:** VoC data should be able to challenge the highest-paid person's opinion

---

## Key Takeaways

- **VoC is not a survey.** It's a system: collect → synthesize → distribute → govern.
- **Your best copy is already written** — by your customers, in reviews and interviews.
- **JTBD unlocks emotional and social jobs** that feature-led messaging misses entirely.
- **Frequency beats perfection.** Four imperfect interviews per month beat one perfect annual study.
- **Synthesize with your team.** VoC locked in a researcher's Notion page has zero impact.

# Part 10: Competitor Motion Analysis — Ads, SEO, Launches & Funnels

## Learning Objectives
- Build a systematic competitive intelligence framework
- Use ad spy tools to decode competitor messaging and budget signals
- Conduct SEO gap analysis to find content and keyword opportunities
- Reverse-engineer competitor funnels to understand their full conversion strategy
- Build a living war room dashboard that keeps your team continuously informed

---

## Why Competitor Motion Analysis Matters

Your competitors are running experiments 24/7 — on messaging, channels, offers, and positioning. Their public behavior (ads, content, launches, pricing pages) is a free R&D feed. Systematic analysis turns that signal into strategy.

**The goal is not to copy competitors.** It's to:
- Understand the market's current messaging assumptions
- Find gaps they're not addressing
- Anticipate their next moves
- Make smarter positioning decisions

---

## The Competitive Intelligence Framework

Structure your CI program around five motion types:

| Motion | What to Track | Tools |
|---|---|---|
| **Ad creative** | Messaging, offers, targeting signals | Meta Ad Library, SpyFu, SimilarWeb |
| **SEO footprint** | Keywords, content strategy, backlinks | Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz |
| **Funnel architecture** | Landing pages, CTAs, nurture sequences | Manual walkthroughs, BuiltWith |
| **Launch patterns** | Product releases, PR, campaign launches | Product Hunt, press, email capture |
| **Positioning** | How they frame themselves vs. category | Website, sales decks, case studies |

## Ad Spy Tools and Techniques

### Top Tools
- **Meta Ad Library** (free) — see all active Facebook/Instagram ads with date launched
- **Google Ads Transparency Center** (free) — search and display ads
- **AdBeat / BigSpy** — cross-platform ad intelligence with creative history
- **SpyFu** — Google Ads keyword bidding history

### What to Look For

**Volume signals:**
- How many ad variants are running? (High count = active testing)
- How long has an ad been running? (Long-running = winning creative)

**Messaging signals:**
- What headline formula are they using? (Problem-agitate, outcome-first, social proof)
- What offer structure? (Free trial, demo, discount, lead magnet)
- What audience signals? (B2B titles, pain-specific language, retargeting copy)

**Creative signals:**
- Video vs. static ratio
- UGC vs. polished production
- Testimonial volume

> **Example:** If a competitor has been running the same testimonial video ad for 90 days, that's a proven performer. Study the format, the structure, and the claim — then find your differentiated version.

## SEO Gap Analysis

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Export competitor keyword rankings** from Ahrefs or SEMrush (top 500 by traffic)
2. **Filter to commercial-intent keywords** (buyer language, comparisons, "best X," "X pricing")
3. **Cross-reference with your own rankings** — find keywords they rank for that you don't
4. **Bucket by funnel stage:**
   - TOFU: educational, category-level
   - MOFU: comparison, "alternative to," feature-specific
   - BOFU: pricing, reviews, "buy X," branded + category

5. **Prioritize by:** Volume × Difficulty × Funnel Stage weight

### Backlink Gap Analysis
- Export competitor backlink profiles
- Find high-authority domains linking to them but not you
- Identify patterns: which types of content earn links? (Tools, guides, data studies, PR)

## Reverse-Engineering Competitor Funnels

### The Manual Walkthrough Process

1. **Enter via paid ad** — click their ad (not your own) to see the full landing page experience
2. **Opt into their lead magnet** — use a dedicated research email address
3. **Map the nurture sequence** — document every email: timing, subject line, CTA
4. **Take the demo/trial** — understand their onboarding and activation logic
5. **Track retargeting behavior** — what ads follow you after abandonment?

### What to Document

| Stage | What to Capture |
|---|---|
| Landing page | Headline, offer, social proof, objection handling |
| Lead magnet | Topic, format, quality, follow-up ask |
| Email sequence | Frequency, tone, content mix, conversion path |
| Demo/trial | Framing, questions asked, value demonstration |
| Objection handling | How they address price, trust, switching cost |

## Launch Pattern Analysis

### Tracking Competitor Launches

- **Product Hunt** — monitor their page and launch activity
- **G2/Capterra reviews** — spike in reviews = likely active launch campaign
- **Press/PR** — Google Alerts for brand name + "announces"
- **LinkedIn posts** — employee advocacy spikes signal launches
- **Job postings** — new hires in specific areas signal strategic shifts

### Reading Launch Patterns

Look for:
- **Seasonal timing** — do they launch at specific times of year?
- **Offer patterns** — do they use discounts, bundles, or feature launches?
- **PR vs. paid** — are launches organic-first or paid-amplified?
- **Follow-up cadence** — how long does a launch campaign run before going dark?

## Competitive Positioning Mapping

### Build a Positioning Matrix

Plot competitors on a 2×2 across the two axes that matter most to your buyers (e.g., Ease-of-use vs. Power, Price vs. Customization).

| | **High Power** | **Low Power** |
|---|---|---|
| **Easy to Use** | Your Target Zone | Competitor A |
| **Complex to Use** | Competitor B | Competitor C |

### Messaging Audit
For each competitor, document:
- **Primary claim** (what they lead with)
- **Target persona** (who their copy speaks to)
- **Proof type** (logos, case studies, data, awards)
- **Missing angle** (what they *don't* talk about — this is your opportunity)

## Building a War Room Dashboard

A war room dashboard is a living document (not a one-time report) maintained weekly.

### Dashboard Components

| Section | Contents | Update Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Active Ads** | Screenshots + duration + key claims | Weekly |
| **SEO Movements** | Ranking gains/losses | Weekly |
| **Funnel Changes** | Landing page updates, offer changes | Monthly |
| **Launch Log** | Recent product/content launches | As-needed |
| **Positioning Map** | Current competitive landscape | Quarterly |

**Tool options:** Notion database, Airtable, or a dedicated Slack channel with automated alerts (Google Alerts, Ahrefs alerts, Mention.com)

## Ethical Competitive Intelligence Practices

Competitive intel is legal and standard — with limits.

### What's Acceptable
- Viewing public ads, websites, and content
- Signing up for public trials or newsletters with your own email
- Analyzing public SEO data via third-party tools
- Reading public reviews, job postings, and press releases

### What to Avoid
- Misrepresenting yourself to extract confidential information
- Accessing competitor systems without authorization
- Using information obtained from former competitor employees that's clearly proprietary
- Sharing competitor's confidential data externally

## Key Takeaways

- **Long-running ads = proven winners.** Don't ignore what's working for competitors — understand *why* it works.
- **SEO gap analysis reveals demand you're not capturing.** Commercial-intent keywords are the highest priority.
- **Funnel walkthroughs are free strategy audits.** Do them manually, not just with tools.
- **Launch patterns predict future moves.** Track cadence, not just individual events.
- **Your war room only has value if it's updated and shared.** Make it a team ritual, not a researcher's artifact.

# Part 13: Demand Creation Audit — New Demand Channels

## Learning Objectives
- Define demand creation and distinguish it from demand capture
- Audit your current demand creation channels for effectiveness
- Select the right channels for your audience and stage
- Use content, social, and community to build category awareness
- Measure demand creation using leading indicators that predict future pipeline

---

## What Demand Creation Actually Is

Demand creation is the process of building awareness, desire, and urgency in audiences who are not yet actively searching for your solution. You are not capturing existing demand — you are *manufacturing* it.

This requires a different mindset:
- **Demand capture** finds buyers ready to decide. Demand creation reaches people before that moment.
- **Success is measured in attention and behavior change**, not immediate conversions.
- **The time horizon is 3–18 months**, not days or weeks.

> The brands that win in saturated markets are often those that created the category before competitors caught up. Slack didn't capture demand for "team messaging software" — it created demand for a new way of working.

---

## Why Demand Creation Requires an Audit

Most companies in early or mid-growth stages underinvest in demand creation — they're too focused on capturing intent that already exists. The audit identifies:
- Which demand creation channels are you currently using?
- Which are producing audience growth, engagement, and pipeline influence?
- Which are consuming budget without building anything durable?
- What gaps exist relative to where your audience spends attention?

## Building Awareness in Cold Audiences

Cold audiences have no relationship with you, no awareness of your category, and no reason to care. Demand creation for cold audiences requires:

### The Three Conditions for Cold Audience Awareness

1. **Relevance** — You must appear in their feed/world in a context they care about *before* introducing your product.
2. **Repetition** — Awareness requires multiple touches. The 7-touch rule is outdated, but frequency matters.
3. **Resonance** — The content must be genuinely useful or entertaining on its own — not a product pitch with a thin editorial veneer.

### Cold Audience Content Formats That Work

| Format | Why It Works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Opinion/POV content** | Strong takes create strong reactions and shares | "Why MQLs are a vanity metric" |
| **Data studies** | Original data earns links, coverage, and authority | "State of [Industry] Report" |
| **Educational frameworks** | Frameworks are shareable and position you as expert | "The 4-stage pipeline model" |
| **Problem-naming content** | Naming a problem your audience has but hasn't articulated creates resonance | "The hidden cost of manual reporting" |
| **Customer stories** | Aspirational use cases make abstract outcomes real | "How [Customer] 3x'd their pipeline" |

## Channel Selection for Demand Creation

Not every channel works for every audience. Use this selection framework:

### Channel-Audience Fit Matrix

| Audience Type | Best Demand Creation Channels |
|---|---|
| B2B executives (VP+) | LinkedIn, industry events, tier-1 press, executive roundtables |
| B2B practitioners (managers/ICs) | LinkedIn, YouTube, newsletters, communities (Slack/Discord), webinars |
| SMB owners | Facebook/Instagram, YouTube, podcasts, SEO-driven blog |
| Consumers | TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Reddit, influencer partnerships |
| Technical buyers (developers, data) | Twitter/X, developer communities, GitHub, conference talks, technical blog |

### Channel Selection Criteria

Before committing to a new demand creation channel, validate:
- **Audience presence:** Are your buyers actually here?
- **Content surface area:** Can you consistently produce content that performs on this channel?
- **Compounding value:** Does effort on this channel build lasting assets (SEO, subscribers, community members)?
- **Resource requirement:** What does consistent execution actually cost in time and money?

## Content as Demand Creation

Content is not inherently demand creation — a product feature announcement or pricing page is not demand creation. Demand creation content does one of three things:

1. **Names a pain** the audience didn't have words for
2. **Expands belief** about what's possible
3. **Positions a category** the audience should care about

### The Content-to-Demand Flywheel

```
Publish high-quality POV content
→ Audience engages and shares
→ ICP followers grow
→ Retargeting and warm audience builds
→ Content warms cold traffic into demo-ready prospects
→ Pipeline from previously cold audience
```

**The flywheel only spins if content quality is high enough to earn organic sharing.** Average content doesn't create demand — it just adds noise.

## Social and Community-Driven Demand

### LinkedIn Organic (B2B Primary Channel)

- **Employee advocacy** outperforms brand pages by 5–8x reach per post
- **Founder/executive voice** builds category authority faster than branded content
- **Content type priorities:** POV posts > Educational carousels > Case study threads > Product announcements
- **Consistency beats virality.** 3 posts/week for 12 months compounds faster than 1 viral post.

### Community-Driven Demand

Owned communities (Slack groups, Discord servers, forums) create demand in a unique way: they build belonging before buying.

**Community demand creation signals:**
- New members joining organically (community is solving a real problem)
- Members referring others to the community
- Community discussions that naturally reference your product
- Events or AMAs that attract non-customers

## Measuring Demand Creation (Leading Indicators)

Demand creation has a lag — you won't see conversions for months. Track leading indicators instead of waiting for lagging revenue metrics.

### Leading Indicators by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Reach** | Organic impressions / Share of voice | Are we becoming visible? |
| **Engagement** | Saves, shares, replies (not just likes) | Are we creating resonance? |
| **Audience growth** | Newsletter subscribers, social followers (ICP-filtered) | Are we building a retargetable audience? |
| **Content consumption** | Blog time-on-page, video watch time, podcast completions | Are we actually educating buyers? |
| **Warm pipeline influence** | % of closed-won deals with prior content touch | Is awareness converting to pipeline? |
| **Branded search volume** | Month-over-month change in branded queries | Are people seeking us out? |

### The Branded Search Test
Branded search volume is one of the best lagging indicators of demand creation success. If your content, social, and community programs are working, more people should be Googling your brand name over time. Track this monthly in Search Console.

## Balancing Demand Capture vs. Creation Budget

There's no universal rule, but here are evidence-based starting points:

| Stage | Capture % | Creation % | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-PMF | 80% | 20% | Validate before scaling |
| Post-PMF, pre-scale | 70% | 30% | Optimize capture, begin building awareness engine |
| Growth stage | 60% | 40% | Capture is efficient; creation builds moat |
| Category leader | 50% | 50% | Category defense requires brand investment |

**The Binet & Field 60/40 Rule** (from B2C research, adapted for B2B): Roughly 60% brand-building (demand creation), 40% activation (demand capture) for long-term growth. Most B2B companies are inverted from this.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Demand creation is an investment, not an expense.** It compounds over 12–24 months.
- **Cold audience content must stand on its own.** If it only works as an ad, it's not demand creation.
- **Leading indicators are your real scorecard.** Don't kill demand creation programs because they don't show immediate ROI.
- **Channel selection is strategic.** One channel done excellently beats five channels done mediocrely.
- **Branded search is the ultimate lagging indicator.** More people seeking you out = demand creation working.

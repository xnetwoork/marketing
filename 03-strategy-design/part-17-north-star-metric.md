# Part 17: North Star Metric & Revenue Tree

## Learning Objectives
- Define a meaningful North Star Metric (NSM) for your business model
- Build a revenue tree that connects leading indicators to revenue outcomes
- Cascade the NSM into team-level OKRs and daily dashboards
- Identify high-leverage points within the revenue tree

---

## What Is a North Star Metric?

A **North Star Metric** is the single metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to customers. It sits at the intersection of customer success and business health — when it grows, revenue follows. When it stalls, churn and decline follow.

> "The NSM is not a vanity metric. It is the metric that, if optimised relentlessly, produces durable, compounding growth." — Sean Ellis, Hacking Growth

### Characteristics of a Strong NSM
- **Leading indicator** of long-term revenue, not a lagging financial result
- **Actionable** — every team can influence it
- **Measurable** — trackable weekly or monthly
- **Customer-centric** — reflects value delivered, not just internal activity
- **Single** — one metric forces prioritisation

---

## NSM by Business Model

| Business Model | Example NSM | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **SaaS (B2B)** | Weekly Active Seats | Predicts retention, expansion, and NRR |
| **SaaS (PLG)** | Activated Users in First 7 Days | Leads to conversion and stickiness |
| **E-commerce** | Orders per Active Buyer per Month | Captures repeat purchase behaviour |
| **Marketplace** | Completed Transactions per Week | Both sides of supply/demand validated |
| **Media/Content** | Daily Active Readers × Time on Site | Monetisation-correlated engagement |
| **Services** | Delivered Engagements at Target NPS | Quality + volume = referral flywheel |

## The Revenue Tree Framework

The revenue tree is a **structured decomposition** of your NSM down to the individual inputs teams can directly influence. It transforms a single number into an action map.

### Structure

```
Revenue
└── NSM (e.g., Weekly Active Seats)
    ├── New User Acquisition
    │   ├── Paid traffic volume
    │   ├── Organic / SEO sessions
    │   └── Referral invites sent
    ├── Activation Rate
    │   ├── Onboarding completion %
    │   └── Time-to-first-value
    ├── Retention / Engagement
    │   ├── Feature adoption depth
    │   └── Support ticket deflection
    └── Expansion
        ├── Seat additions per account
        └── Upsell conversion rate
```

Each branch of the tree is a **lever**. The tree reveals which levers have the most revenue sensitivity.

### Building Your Revenue Tree — Step by Step

1. **Start with revenue** — ARR, GMV, or net revenue depending on your model
2. **Identify the 2–4 multiplicative factors** that produce it (e.g., Revenue = ARPU × Active Users)
3. **Decompose each factor** into the leading behaviours that drive it
4. **Tag ownership** — assign each leaf node to a team or squad
5. **Quantify sensitivity** — a 10% improvement in which node moves revenue the most?

## Connecting Leading Indicators to Revenue

A common failure: teams track revenue but optimise for the wrong inputs. The revenue tree forces you to map **correlation → causation → leverage**.

### Leverage Point Analysis

Run a simple regression or use historical cohort data to answer:
- Which leading indicator has the strongest correlation with 90-day revenue?
- Which indicator is most under-invested relative to its impact?
- Which indicator is at or near a ceiling (saturation)?

**Example — B2B SaaS:**
- Activation rate (completing setup in Week 1) predicts 12-month retention with 0.74 correlation
- A 10-point improvement in activation → 18% reduction in 6-month churn → $1.2M NRR impact
- Current activation rate: 42% — significant room to move

This is a leverage point. It becomes the team's quarterly focus.

## Common NSM Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Choosing a revenue metric as NSM | Lags too much; teams can't act on it | Use a leading customer behaviour metric |
| Choosing an activity metric (emails sent, calls made) | Optimises inputs, not outcomes | Pick a metric the *customer* cares about |
| Using multiple NSMs | Dilutes focus; creates internal politics | One NSM, with supporting sub-metrics |
| Never revisiting the NSM | Wrong metric for your current growth stage | Review NSM annually or at major pivots |
| NSM not visible to every team | Disconnect between work and impact | Dashboard NSM on every team's standup screen |

## Cascading NSM to Team OKRs

The NSM provides strategic direction. OKRs translate it into quarterly execution.

### Cascade Model

```
Company NSM: Weekly Active Seats → 50,000 by Q4

Growth Team OKR:
  Objective: Accelerate new user acquisition
  KR1: Increase paid trial starts from 2,400 to 3,200/month
  KR2: Improve landing page CVR from 4.1% to 5.5%

Product Team OKR:
  Objective: Drive activation to unlock retention
  KR1: Raise 7-day activation rate from 42% to 58%
  KR2: Reduce time-to-first-value from 6 days to 3 days

CS Team OKR:
  Objective: Protect and expand existing seats
  KR1: Increase net seat expansion rate to 112%
  KR2: Reduce 90-day churn from 8% to 5%
```

Each team's OKRs **directly feed** the revenue tree nodes that contribute to the NSM.

## Using the Revenue Tree to Find Leverage Points

### The 80/20 Diagnostic
Run a sensitivity analysis: hold all variables constant and move each node by +10%. Which one moves the NSM the most? That is your **current leverage point**.

### Bottleneck Mapping
Look for the node where improvement has stalled or where the conversion rate is significantly below industry benchmark. A bottleneck upstream limits every node downstream.

### Constraint Theory Applied to Growth
- **Identify the constraint** (lowest-converting or highest-drop-off node)
- **Exploit it** — focus resources here exclusively
- **Subordinate** other improvements until the constraint is resolved
- **Elevate** — invest to structurally improve throughput
- **Repeat** — find the next constraint

---

## Key Takeaways

- **One NSM, owned by leadership**, visible to everyone
- The revenue tree converts strategy into executable team-level priorities
- Leverage points change over time — run a sensitivity analysis quarterly
- Cascade from NSM → OKRs → sprints to maintain strategic alignment
- The most dangerous metric is the one that looks good but doesn't predict revenue

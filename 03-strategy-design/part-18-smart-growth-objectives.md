# Part 18: SMART Growth Objectives (90/180/365 Days)

## Learning Objectives
- Apply an upgraded SMART framework to marketing strategy
- Design a three-horizon objective system (90/180/365 days)
- Distinguish between OKRs and SMART goals and use each appropriately
- Build accountability systems that survive mid-cycle disruption

---

## Why Objectives Fail (And How to Fix It)

Most growth teams set objectives that are either too vague ("grow revenue"), too output-focused ("run 12 campaigns"), or disconnected from strategy. The result: busy teams, poor outcomes, and no accountability.

A well-designed objective system answers three questions:
- **Where are we going?** (Annual targets)
- **What does progress look like?** (180-day milestones)
- **What do we do right now?** (90-day sprints)

---

## The Upgraded SMART Framework

The original SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) remain valid, but modern growth strategy demands two additions.

| Letter | Classic | Upgraded |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Specific | Specific + **outcome-oriented** (not activity-based) |
| **M** | Measurable | Measurable + **leading and lagging** indicators defined |
| **A** | Achievable | Ambitious-yet-achievable (70% confidence, not 100%) |
| **R** | Relevant | Relevant to **NSM and revenue tree** |
| **T** | Time-bound | Time-bound with **review checkpoints**, not just end dates |

> **The SMART+ rule:** If you can hit the objective without meaningfully growing the business, it's the wrong objective.

### Writing a SMART+ Objective

**Weak:** "Increase website traffic in Q2"

**Strong:** "Increase marketing-qualified organic sessions from 18,400/month to 28,000/month by June 30, as measured by GA4, with a 30-day checkpoint at 22,000 — contributing to our NSM of 50,000 Weekly Active Seats"

## The Three-Horizon Objective System

### Horizon 1 — 90-Day Sprint Objectives

**Purpose:** Focused execution. Short enough to maintain urgency, long enough to see results.

**Characteristics:**
- 3–5 objectives maximum per team
- Fully within the team's control
- Directly tied to a revenue tree lever
- Weekly progress check-ins built in

**Example (Growth Team, Q1):**
- Grow paid trial volume from 2,400 to 3,200/month by March 31
- Improve trial-to-paid conversion from 18% to 24% by March 31
- Launch referral programme and generate 400 referred signups by March 31

**Design principle:** 90-day sprints should feel *slightly uncomfortable*. If the team is confident they'll hit it, the target isn't ambitious enough.

---

### Horizon 2 — 180-Day Milestones

**Purpose:** Strategic checkpoints. Validate that 90-day sprints are accumulating into meaningful progress.

**Characteristics:**
- 2–3 milestones per function
- Set at the start of the half-year
- Connect sprint outputs to strategy
- Trigger resource reallocation if off-track

**Example (Marketing, H1):**
- Achieve 40% of annual pipeline target by June 30
- Establish SEO as a top-3 traffic source (currently #5) by June 30
- Launch in 2 new geographic markets with initial demand signals validated

**Design principle:** 180-day milestones are strategic gates. Missing one should prompt a strategy review, not just a pep talk.

---

### Horizon 3 — Annual Growth Targets

**Purpose:** Directional ambition. Sets the context for all shorter-horizon objectives.

**Characteristics:**
- Set by leadership with cross-functional input
- 3–5 company-level targets maximum
- Include both growth and efficiency metrics
- Reviewed quarterly, not changed lightly

**Example (Company, Full Year):**
- Grow ARR from $4.2M to $7.0M
- Achieve payback period under 18 months
- Reach NSM of 50,000 Weekly Active Seats
- Expand to 3 new verticals with at least $200K ARR each

## OKRs vs SMART Goals

Many teams are confused about when to use OKRs versus SMART goals. They serve different purposes.

| Dimension | OKRs | SMART Goals |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary use** | Alignment and ambition | Accountability and clarity |
| **Structure** | Objective + 3–5 Key Results | Single objective with criteria |
| **Ambition** | Moonshot (60–70% achievable) | Realistic with stretch |
| **Frequency** | Quarterly or annual | Project, campaign, or quarterly |
| **Best for** | Cross-team strategic alignment | Individual or team execution |
| **Failure posture** | 70% achievement = success | Miss = problem to diagnose |

**Recommended practice:** Use **annual OKRs for company and department alignment**, then **SMART+ goals for 90-day sprint execution**. They are complementary, not competing.

## Aligning Objectives Across Departments

Misaligned objectives are one of the largest sources of wasted effort in growth organisations. Marketing optimises for leads while Sales optimises for deals — and the handoff breaks.

### Alignment Protocol

1. **Start with the company NSM** — all departments must connect their objectives to it
2. **Map dependencies** — which team's output is another team's input? Make that explicit
3. **Hold a shared kickoff** — cross-functional 90-day planning session (not siloed)
4. **Shared KRs where appropriate** — Marketing and Sales can co-own pipeline quality metrics
5. **Conflict resolution upfront** — surface trade-offs before the quarter begins

**Example alignment failure and fix:**
- ❌ Marketing objective: "Increase MQL volume by 40%" — leads to MQL inflation
- ❌ Sales objective: "Close 35 new logos" — leads to cherry-picking, not process
- ✅ Shared objective: "Generate 35 net new customers from marketing-sourced pipeline at ≥22% win rate"

## Building Accountability Systems

Setting objectives is easy. Maintaining accountability through a 90-day sprint is hard. Build the system before the quarter starts.

### The Accountability Stack

| Layer | Mechanism | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Individual** | Personal objective scorecard | Weekly self-review |
| **Team** | Progress tracking dashboard | Weekly standup review |
| **Leadership** | Objective health report (RAG status) | Bi-weekly |
| **Cross-functional** | Shared OKR review | Monthly |
| **Board/Executive** | Strategic milestone review | Quarterly |

### RAG Status Definitions
- 🟢 **Green:** On track or ahead of plan
- 🟡 **Amber:** At risk — needs attention and a mitigation plan within 5 business days
- 🔴 **Red:** Off track — requires executive decision on resource or objective change

**Critical rule:** Amber and Red statuses must surface immediately, not at the end-of-quarter review.

## Reviewing and Adjusting Objectives Mid-Cycle

Rigid objectives in a volatile market are a liability. Build in structured review points.

### Mid-Cycle Review Checklist (Day 45 of 90)
- [ ] Are we at approximately 50% of the target metric?
- [ ] Have external conditions changed materially? (Competitive action, economic shift, algorithm update)
- [ ] Is the objective still strategically relevant?
- [ ] Are there resource constraints blocking progress?
- [ ] Has the team learned something that changes the approach?

### Adjustment Decision Framework

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Ahead of plan | Consider raising the target, or reallocate resources to lagging objectives |
| On track with headwinds | Document risks, maintain target |
| Behind due to execution | Fix the execution, not the objective |
| Behind due to external change | Adjust objective, document learning |
| Wrong objective identified | Reset with team alignment — do not silently drop it |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Three horizons** (90/180/365) give teams both urgency and direction
- SMART+ adds outcome-orientation and review cadence to the classic framework
- OKRs align ambition; SMART goals drive accountable execution
- Cross-functional alignment must happen *before* the quarter, not during
- The accountability system is more important than the objective document

## Actionable Next Steps

1. **This week:** Audit your current objectives against the SMART+ criteria — rewrite any that are activity-based
2. **Before next quarter:** Run a cross-functional objective alignment session with a shared NSM as the anchor
3. **Day 1 of each quarter:** Publish objectives in a visible, shared tracker with RAG status columns
4. **Day 45 of each quarter:** Mandatory mid-cycle review — adjust or confirm all objectives
5. **Day 90:** Retrospective — score objectives, extract learnings, carry forward into the next cycle

# Part 21: Defensive vs Offensive Resource Split

## Learning Objectives
- Apply a strategic framework for allocating resources between offensive and defensive plays
- Identify when market conditions call for attack vs protection
- Design offensive and defensive playbooks for your competitive context
- Build a budget split model calibrated to your business stage

---

## The Strategic Resource Allocation Problem

Every marketing budget allocation decision is implicitly a choice between **growing share** (offensive) and **protecting share** (defensive). Most companies don't make this choice explicitly — they inherit last year's budget with incremental adjustments, leading to misalignment between strategy and spend.

**The core trade-off:**
- Offensive investment captures new customers, enters new markets, and grows the top line — but requires time and carries risk
- Defensive investment retains existing customers, builds switching costs, and protects margin — but alone, it can't grow a business

The goal is not to choose one over the other. It is to allocate deliberately based on your competitive position and growth stage.

---

## When to Defend vs When to Attack

### Attack When:
- You have a product advantage competitors cannot easily copy
- A competitor is weakened (funding issues, product problems, bad press)
- A new segment or geography is underserved and early entrants win
- Your current market share is below 20% — significant penetration upside remains
- You have a customer acquisition cost advantage that allows you to outpace incumbents

### Defend When:
- Your churn rate is above industry benchmark — plugging the bucket before filling it
- A well-funded competitor has entered your core market
- You have a strong market position (>40% share) that is more valuable to protect than to expand
- Customer acquisition costs are rising and retention ROI is improving
- You are approaching a fundraise or acquisition — unit economics must be clean

### Mixed Signal Situations
Most companies operate in ambiguity. Use this diagnostic:

| Signal | Direction |
|---|---|
| NRR < 100% | Tilt defensive — stop the bleed first |
| NRR > 110% | Tilt offensive — growth machine is working |
| CAC increasing >20% YoY | Investigate before adding offensive spend |
| Market growing >20% YoY | Tilt offensive — land grab before consolidation |
| Competitor raising large round | Defensive accelerate: lock in customers, deepen moats |

## Offensive Plays

### 1. Conquesting
Directly targeting competitor customers through advertising, pricing, and migration offers.

**Tactics:**
- Competitor brand keyword bidding (PPC)
- Comparison landing pages (honest, specific, defensible)
- Migration tooling and white-glove switch support
- "Why customers leave [Competitor]" content for SEO
- Outbound sequences targeting competitor renewal windows

**Success metric:** Competitive win rate improvement + new logo CAC from competitive segment

---

### 2. New Market Entry
Moving into adjacent segments or geographies before competitors establish a position.

**Tactics:**
- Stage-gate expansion (as covered in Part 20)
- First-mover content and SEO in emerging segments
- Partnership-led distribution in new markets
- Category creation — define the problem in terms that favour your solution

---

### 3. Aggressive Growth Mode
When conditions are favourable, prioritise speed over efficiency.

**Signals to activate aggressive growth:**
- Proven product-market fit with strong activation and retention
- CAC payback under 12 months at current scale
- Market window is time-limited (regulatory, seasonal, competitive)

**Tactics:**
- Increase paid media spend significantly (2–3x)
- Expand sales team faster than pipeline justifies (demand-led hiring)
- Invest in category-level brand campaigns to shape perception
- Offer aggressive introductory pricing to accelerate trial

---

## Defensive Plays

### 1. Retention Programs
The highest-ROI investment for any business with existing customers.

**Tactics:**
- Customer success investment proportional to account value
- Proactive health scoring and early churn intervention
- Regular business reviews for key accounts
- Product adoption campaigns to increase feature depth and switching cost
- Loyalty and renewal incentive programmes

**Benchmark:** Every 1% reduction in monthly churn compounds dramatically — a business with 2% monthly churn has a 12-month LTV 40% higher than one with 3%.

---

### 2. Building Moats

Moats are structural advantages that make it harder for competitors to take your customers or your market position.

| Moat Type | How to Build It |
|---|---|
| **Data moat** | Accumulate proprietary data that improves your product over time |
| **Network effects** | Design features where value increases with user count |
| **Switching costs** | Deep integrations, data migration friction, embedded workflows |
| **Brand moat** | Consistent investment in brand equity and category leadership |
| **Distribution moat** | Exclusive partnerships, embedded channels, platform integrations |

**Defensive investment in moats pays dividends over years, not quarters.** It rarely shows up in 90-day metrics but changes the competitive landscape fundamentally.

---

### 3. Brand Defence

When competitors attack your brand position — through comparative advertising, negative PR, or aggressive positioning — a defensive brand response is required.

**Tactics:**
- Increase share of voice in your core category at critical moments
- Publish thought leadership that reinforces your positioning narrative
- Mobilise customer advocates to provide social proof at scale
- Address competitor claims directly and factually (don't be vague)
- Protect organic search rankings on brand terms and category terms

## Budget Split Models by Business Stage

These are starting points, not rigid formulas. Adjust based on your NRR, competitive intensity, and market growth rate.

| Stage | Offensive % | Defensive % | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-PMF** | 30% | 70% | Understand and retain existing customers; don't scale acquisition yet |
| **Early growth** (PMF found, <$5M ARR) | 70% | 30% | Land-grab mode; grow fast before competitors arrive |
| **Growth stage** ($5M–$50M ARR) | 60% | 40% | Balance growth with building retention infrastructure |
| **Scale stage** ($50M+ ARR) | 50% | 50% | Defend existing base while pursuing adjacent expansion |
| **Market leader** (>40% share) | 40% | 60% | Protect dominant position; selective offensive plays in adjacencies |

### Budget Split Adjustment Triggers

Move **more offensive** if:
- New well-funded competitor just entered your market
- You are closing a funding round and must demonstrate growth
- A new distribution channel has opened with first-mover advantage

Move **more defensive** if:
- NRR has declined two consecutive quarters
- A large enterprise customer is at risk
- Your brand NPS has dropped significantly
- CAC efficiency has declined >25% without explanation

## Competitive Response Strategies

When a specific competitive event occurs, you need a response protocol.

| Event | Recommended Response |
|---|---|
| Competitor launches a lower-price tier | Do NOT immediately match; analyse if they're targeting your ICP. Respond with value messaging, not price cut |
| Competitor launches a feature you don't have | Accelerate product roadmap; bridge with service/workaround communication; be honest with customers |
| Competitor raises large funding round | Lock in key account renewals early; intensify customer success; review your own funding timeline |
| Competitor runs aggressive conquesting campaign | Increase retention investment in at-risk accounts; respond with social proof and comparison content |
| Key competitor loses a major customer | Activate targeted outreach — this is a conquest opportunity |

---

## Monitoring Competitive Threats

You cannot respond to threats you don't see. Build a competitive intelligence system.

### Monitoring Stack
- **Review sites** (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot) — set alerts for competitor review patterns
- **Job postings** — competitor hiring tells you where they're investing
- **Pricing pages** — monitor for changes (use automated monitoring tools)
- **Content and SEO** — track competitor content output and keyword rankings
- **Social listening** — monitor mentions of competitors across social channels
- **Customer feedback** — ask in win/loss calls which competitors were considered

### Competitive Intelligence Cadence

| Activity | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Competitor pricing/positioning review | Monthly |
| Win/loss analysis with sales team | Monthly |
| Competitor content and SEO audit | Quarterly |
| Full competitive landscape review | Semi-annually |
| Customer sentiment on competitive alternatives | Ongoing (via NPS surveys, CS notes) |

---

## Key Takeaways

- Offensive and defensive investment must be balanced deliberately — not by default
- Business stage and NRR are the primary inputs to your budget split
- Moat-building is defensive investment with compounding long-term returns
- Competitive response must be fast and specific — vague defensive positioning loses
- You need a live competitive intelligence system, not an annual audit

---

## Actionable Next Steps

1. **Calculate your current offensive/defensive split** — categorise every budget line item
2. **Run the diagnostic** — is your NRR and churn rate signalling a need to tilt defensive?
3. **Identify your #1 moat to build** — which structural advantage will matter most in 3 years?
4. **Set up a competitive monitoring stack** — at minimum, G2 alerts, job posting tracking, and a pricing monitor
5. **Design a competitive response protocol** — assign ownership so responses happen in days, not weeks

# Part 26: 12-Week Blitz Calendar

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the theory of concentrated effort and why it outperforms year-round even spend
- Build a complete 12-week blitz calendar from scratch
- Execute pre-blitz preparation across creative, landing pages, and budgets
- Apply week-by-week tactical templates to any blitz campaign
- Manage team energy and prevent burnout during high-intensity periods

---

## The Theory of Concentrated Effort

Most marketing plans spread effort evenly across 52 weeks. This produces mediocre results everywhere. The **12-week blitz** applies the Pareto principle to time: concentrate your best people, biggest budgets, and sharpest creative into a focused sprint to achieve outsized results.

**Why 12 weeks?**
- Long enough to see compound learning effects in paid media
- Short enough to maintain urgency and team energy
- Aligned with quarterly business planning cycles
- Provides sufficient data for statistically valid decisions

> **Real-World Example:** Shopify runs focused merchant acquisition blitzes aligned to key commerce seasons (Q4, BFCM, New Year). Rather than flat spend, they 3–5x budget in concentrated windows, capturing disproportionate market share when buyer intent peaks.

**The Blitz Mindset:** A blitz is not "work harder." It's **work with complete focus on one objective** while temporarily deprioritizing everything else.

## Building a 12-Week Blitz Calendar

### Step 1: Define the Blitz Objective

Choose **one primary objective** (not three):
- Acquire X new customers at ≤$Y CAC
- Generate $X in revenue from a specific segment
- Achieve X% market share in a specific channel or region
- Launch a new product to X% of target addressable market

### Step 2: Set the Blitz Window

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-Blitz Prep | Weeks -4 to -1 | Creative, pages, budgets, team briefing |
| Launch Acceleration | Weeks 1–3 | Rapid testing, establish winners |
| Optimization Sprint | Weeks 4–8 | Scale winners, kill losers, iterate |
| Final Push | Weeks 9–11 | Max budget deployment, urgency campaigns |
| Wind-Down & Analysis | Week 12 | Budget taper, data harvest, retrospective |

### Step 3: Allocate Blitz Budget

**Recommended budget structure:**
- **60%** to proven channels (already generating positive ROAS)
- **25%** to channel experiments (structured tests with clear kill criteria)
- **15%** held in reserve for scaling surprise winners mid-blitz

---

## Pre-Blitz Preparation (Weeks -4 to -1)

Nothing kills a blitz faster than scrambling for creative on Week 1. Pre-blitz prep is the difference between a controlled offensive and a chaotic scramble.

**Week -4: Strategy Lock**
- Finalize objective, KPIs, and success metrics
- Assign war room roles and decision authorities
- Map audience segments and channel strategy

**Week -3: Creative Production**
- Brief all creative assets: ads, emails, landing pages, social content
- Produce 3x more creative than you think you'll need
- Pre-build creative variants for key audience segments

**Week -2: Technical Setup**
- Build and QA all landing pages
- Configure tracking (pixels, UTMs, conversion events)
- Set up dashboards and automated reporting
- Brief sales team on expected lead volume and quality

**Week -1: Final Checks**
- Soft-launch campaigns at 10% budget to catch tracking issues
- Review all creative for brand compliance and legal approval
- Confirm budget approvals and payment methods
- Team briefing: roles, escalation paths, daily cadence

## Week-by-Week Execution Template

### Weeks 1–3: Launch Acceleration

**Weekly objectives:**
- Deploy 8–12 ad creative variants per primary channel
- Test 2–3 landing page variants per key segment
- Establish baseline CPL/CPA benchmarks
- Identify top 20% of creatives by hook rate and CVR

**Daily actions:**
- Morning: Pull overnight data, identify anomalies
- Midday: Optimization decisions (pause underperformers, shift budget)
- EOD: Creative queue review, brief replacements for paused assets

**Kill criteria (pause if):**
- CPL > 2x target after 200+ impressions
- CTR < 0.5% for cold audiences after 1,000 impressions
- Landing page CVR < 1% after 500 sessions

### Weeks 4–8: Optimization Sprint

**Weekly objectives:**
- Scale winning creative to 3x original budget
- Run A/B tests on landing page elements (headline, CTA, social proof)
- Expand winning audiences with lookalikes and adjacent targeting
- Introduce retargeting sequences for non-converters

**Optimization cadence:**
| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | Weekly KPI review, budget reallocation |
| Tuesday–Thursday | Incremental optimizations (bids, audiences, creative) |
| Friday | Creative performance review, brief new variants |

### Weeks 9–11: Final Push

**Tactics:**
- Deploy urgency and scarcity messaging ("Final Days," "Limited Spots")
- Launch retargeting blitz to all warm audiences from Weeks 1–8
- Email re-engagement to unconverted leads from earlier in blitz
- Increase bid aggressiveness on highest-intent audiences
- Deploy bonus offers or bundles to overcome remaining objections

### Week 12: Wind-Down and Analysis

**Activities:**
- Gradually reduce spend by 20–30% per day to avoid algorithmic disruption
- Export all performance data before campaign closure
- Run full retrospective: what worked, what didn't, what surprised you
- Document learnings for the next blitz

## Mid-Blitz Optimization Protocol

At **Week 4** and **Week 8**, run a formal mid-blitz review:

**Questions to answer:**
1. Are we on pace to hit our objective? If not, what's the gap?
2. Which creatives are in the top quartile? Are they scaled appropriately?
3. Which channels are over/underperforming vs. forecast?
4. Is team energy sustainable for the remaining weeks?
5. Are there new opportunities (new audiences, new angles) worth testing?

**Mid-blitz pivot triggers:**
- If primary channel CAC exceeds target by >30% at Week 4 → shift budget to secondary channel
- If a new creative concept outperforms champion by >50% → immediately scale it
- If overall pacing is >20% behind target at Week 6 → activate budget reserve

---

## Post-Blitz Analysis

A blitz without a proper retrospective is a wasted learning opportunity.

**Post-Blitz Report Structure:**
1. **Executive summary:** Objective vs. result, key metrics
2. **Channel performance breakdown:** ROAS, CAC, volume by channel
3. **Creative performance:** Top 5 creatives by CVR, hook rate, revenue
4. **Audience insights:** Which segments converted best, CPL by segment
5. **Learnings and surprises:** What you didn't expect
6. **Next blitz recommendations:** What to do differently

---

## Managing Team Energy During a Blitz

Blitzes are sprints, not marathons. Unsustainable pace leads to mistakes, burnout, and attrition.

**Energy management tactics:**
- **Set clear end dates.** Teams push harder when they see the finish line.
- **Rotate on-call duties.** No single person monitors dashboards 24/7.
- **Celebrate weekly wins.** Recognize progress, not just final outcomes.
- **Protect recovery time.** Build the week after the blitz into the plan as a recovery week.
- **Prevent scope creep.** "Just one more thing" kills blitz focus — escalate additions to the next blitz backlog.

## Key Takeaways

- **Concentration beats distribution** — 12 weeks of focused effort outperforms 52 weeks of spread effort
- Pre-blitz preparation is as important as the blitz itself — underprepared teams waste the first 2 weeks scrambling
- Build in formal mid-blitz reviews at Week 4 and Week 8 to course-correct before it's too late
- Manage team energy actively — a burned-out team in Week 10 loses the final push
- A thorough post-blitz analysis is the investment that funds your next blitz's success

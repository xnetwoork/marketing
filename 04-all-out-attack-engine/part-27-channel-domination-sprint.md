## The Channel Domination Principle: Depth vs. Breadth

Most growth teams spread their budgets across 6–8 channels, achieving weak results in all of them. **Channel domination** is the opposite strategy: concentrate resources on 2 primary channels until you achieve measurable leadership, then use a secondary channel for diversification and testing.

**Why this works:**
- Platform algorithms reward consistent, high-volume advertisers with better auction positioning and lower CPMs
- Your creative team can produce better output when focused on fewer formats
- You build proprietary audience data faster with concentrated spend
- Your team develops deep platform expertise that compounds over time

> **Real-World Example:** Dollar Shave Club dominated YouTube and email before expanding to other channels. Warby Parker owned paid search and referral. Concentration produced category-defining results before diversification.

**The Rule of 2+1:**
- **Primary Channel 1:** Highest proven ROAS, largest addressable audience
- **Primary Channel 2:** Complements Primary 1 in funnel stage or audience type
- **Secondary Channel:** Experimental or retargeting, max 15% of total budget

## Criteria for Selecting Primary Channels

Score each candidate channel on these dimensions (1–5 scale):

| Criteria | Description |
|---|---|
| **Audience Fit** | Does your ICP actively use this platform? |
| **Proven ROAS** | Have you achieved positive returns historically? |
| **Volume Potential** | Is the addressable audience large enough to scale? |
| **Creative Capability** | Can your team produce compelling content for this format? |
| **Platform Maturity** | Is the channel algorithm stable and predictable? |
| **Competitive Density** | Is there room to achieve leadership, or is it oversaturated? |

**Scoring guide:** Channels with a combined score of 20+ are strong primary candidates. Channels scoring 14–19 are secondary candidates. Below 14, deprioritize.

**Primary channel pairs that work well together:**

| Primary 1 | Primary 2 | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Meta (broad prospecting) | Google Search (high-intent capture) | Full funnel coverage |
| LinkedIn (B2B awareness) | Google Search (B2B intent) | B2B domination |
| TikTok (awareness, Gen Z) | Meta (retargeting, conversion) | Awareness-to-conversion flow |
| YouTube (education/awareness) | Meta (retargeting) | Content-led growth |

## Secondary Channel Role

The secondary channel serves one of three strategic functions:

1. **Retargeting net:** Recapture users who engaged with primary channels but didn't convert (e.g., email, display, YouTube)
2. **Test-and-learn lab:** Experiment with a new channel at low risk (≤15% budget) to assess future primary potential
3. **Audience extension:** Reach adjacent audiences not accessible via primary channels

**Secondary channel budget constraint:** If it exceeds 15% of total spend, it becomes a primary by default — treat it accordingly.

## Channel Domination Metrics

You don't just want good performance — you want to own the channel in your category.

### Share of Voice (SOV)
- **Definition:** Your brand's impressions as a percentage of total category impressions
- **Target:** >25% SOV in primary channels = strong position; >40% = dominance
- **How to measure:** Competitor intelligence tools (SpyFu, SEMrush, Meta Ad Library)

### Share of Search (SoS)
- **Definition:** Your brand's search volume as a percentage of total category search volume
- **Target:** Track SoS trend over the blitz — upward trend confirms growing mind share
- **How to measure:** Google Trends, SEMrush brand vs. category keyword volume

### CTR Leadership
- **Definition:** Your average CTR vs. industry benchmark for the channel/format
- **Target:** >2x industry benchmark CTR signals creative and audience dominance
- **Meta benchmark CTR:** 0.9–1.5% for feed ads; **domination target:** >2.5%
- **Google Search benchmark CTR:** 5–10% by industry; **domination target:** top position

### Additional Domination KPIs

| Metric | Domination Benchmark |
|---|---|
| CPM vs. category average | ≥20% below category average (signals quality score advantage) |
| Frequency at target CPM | Ability to reach 4+ frequency without CPM spike |
| Auction win rate | >60% of target keyword/audience auctions |
| Creative relevance score | Meta Relevance Score >8; Google Quality Score >8 |

## Tactical Playbooks by Channel

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Domination Playbook

**Setup:**
- Consolidate into 3–5 high-budget ad sets (avoid audience fragmentation)
- Use Advantage+ audience for prospecting, manual audience for retargeting
- Minimum $500/day per ad set for algorithm optimization

**Domination tactics:**
- Test 15+ creative variants in Week 1; scale top 3 by Week 3
- Use video (15–30 sec) for prospecting; static or carousel for retargeting
- Run engagement custom audiences (3-sec video views, page engagers) for mid-funnel

### Google Search Domination Playbook

**Setup:**
- Achieve Top Impression Share >80% for your top 20 commercial keywords
- Bid on all competitor brand terms (conquest campaigns)
- Run RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads) to bid up on known warm audiences

**Domination tactics:**
- Own your brand terms completely — never let competitors outbid you on your own name
- Use RSAs with 15 headlines/4 descriptions; pin best-performing elements after statistical significance
- Maximize ad extensions: sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, image extensions

### LinkedIn Domination Playbook (B2B)

**Setup:**
- Target by job title + company size + industry (not interest-based)
- Minimum 300K audience size per ad set for efficient delivery
- Frequency target: 4–6 per month per user in ICP

**Domination tactics:**
- Lead Gen Forms dramatically outperform external landing pages on LinkedIn
- Thought leadership content (Document Ads, video) builds category authority
- Use Conversation Ads for personalized outreach at scale

### TikTok Domination Playbook

**Setup:**
- Minimum 1M+ audience size; platform penalizes small audiences
- Budget minimum $100/day for Spark Ads, $200/day for standard In-Feed

**Domination tactics:**
- Native-style creative (no polish, authentic feel) dramatically outperforms branded ads
- Hook within first 1–2 seconds is everything — test 5 different hooks on the same concept
- Use TikTok's Creative Center to monitor trending sounds and formats weekly

## Sprint Structure and Exit Criteria

### Sprint Structure (8 Weeks)

| Phase | Weeks | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| Channel validation | 1–2 | Confirm channel viability with initial data |
| Scaling | 3–5 | 3x budget on winners, kill underperformers |
| Domination push | 6–7 | Push SOV and CTR leadership metrics |
| Measurement and decision | 8 | Evaluate exit criteria, decide: continue or rotate |

### Exit Criteria (Move to New Primary Channel When):

- CAC has risen >40% from blitz baseline despite 4+ weeks of optimization
- SOV is plateauing below 20% despite increased investment
- Creative fatigue is reducing CTR month-over-month with no recovery
- A secondary channel is consistently outperforming at lower CAC for 3+ consecutive weeks

## When to Rotate Channels

Channel rotation is a **strategic decision**, not a panic response. Rotate when:

1. **Diminishing returns are structural** — not just a creative issue. If new creative doesn't fix performance, the channel itself is saturated for your offer.
2. **New platform emergence** — early movers on new platforms (TikTok in 2020, LinkedIn video in 2019) earn disproportionate returns.
3. **Audience lifecycle** — your ICP has migrated platforms (e.g., B2B audiences moving from Facebook to LinkedIn over 2016–2020).
4. **Budget efficiency threshold** — another channel offers >30% lower CAC at equivalent volume.

**Rotation protocol:**
- Ramp secondary channel to 30% budget while maintaining primary at 70%
- Wait for 4 weeks of comparable data before flipping allocation
- Never cold-stop a primary channel — gradual ramp-down protects audience data and algorithm momentum

## Key Takeaways

- **Two primary channels, maximum** — spreading thins your results and your team
- Select primary channels based on audience fit, proven ROAS, and creative capability — not trends
- Domination is measurable: track SOV, share of search, and CTR leadership benchmarks
- Each major channel has a distinct algorithm logic — build platform-specific playbooks
- Exit criteria should be defined before the sprint starts, not mid-sprint under pressure
- Rotation is a strategic move based on structural evidence, not a knee-jerk response to a bad week

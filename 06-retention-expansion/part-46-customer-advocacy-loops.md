## The Customer Advocacy Flywheel

Customer advocacy is the highest-trust, lowest-cost acquisition channel available. A satisfied customer who refers a colleague or writes a G2 review does more to accelerate growth than most paid campaigns—and at a fraction of the cost.

**The advocacy flywheel:**
```
Great Product + Great Experience
        ↓
Happy, Successful Customers
        ↓
Referrals + Reviews + UGC + Case Studies
        ↓
New Customers Acquired with High Trust
        ↓
Faster Sales Cycles + Higher Close Rates
        ↓
More Revenue → Better Product → More Happy Customers
```

The key insight: **advocacy is not a campaign—it is a system.** One-time "refer a friend" emails are not advocacy programs. Systematic advocacy programs build compounding assets over time.

## Building a Customer Advocacy Program

### Program Architecture

A mature customer advocacy program has four layers:

| Layer | Activity | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Identify** | Find and score your most enthusiastic customers | Advocacy candidate list |
| **Activate** | Convert enthusiasm into action | Referrals, reviews, testimonials |
| **Amplify** | Distribute advocacy content at scale | Case studies, UGC, community |
| **Reward** | Reinforce advocacy behavior | Recognition, incentives, exclusivity |

### Identifying Advocates
- **NPS promoters (9–10):** Your warmest, most actionable pool
- **Power users:** Heavy product usage signals genuine value received
- **Expansion customers:** Willingness to pay more is a strong satisfaction signal
- **Champions with brand visibility:** Customers who are influential in their industry amplify your brand beyond the sale
- **Long-tenured customers:** 12+ months with no escalations indicates deep satisfaction

### Advocacy Tiers
Structure advocates into tiers with differentiated benefits and asks:

| Tier | Profile | Ask | Reward |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bronze** | NPS 8–10 | Write a review on G2/Capterra | Product credit, gift card |
| **Silver** | Power user, 6+ months | Testimonial quote + referral | Feature access, event invite |
| **Gold** | Champion, reference customer | Case study, speaking opportunity | Co-marketing, executive access |
| **Advisory** | Industry leader, strategic customer | Product advisory board | Equity consideration, strategic partnership |

## Referral Program Design

### Core Mechanics

A referral program must answer three questions clearly:
1. **Who gets rewarded?** (referrer, referee, or both)
2. **What is the reward?** (cash, credit, product, swag)
3. **What triggers the reward?** (sign-up, first payment, 30 days retained)

**Double-sided rewards consistently outperform single-sided programs** because they give the referrer a genuine gift to offer, not just a self-interested incentive.

**Referral program examples:**
- **Dropbox (classic):** 500MB extra storage for referrer AND referee—aligned perfectly with the product's core value
- **Uber:** Account credit for both sides—frictionless, immediately usable
- **B2B SaaS:** 1 month free for referrer + extended trial for referee

### Viral Coefficient (K-Factor)

```
K = Invitations Sent per User × Conversion Rate of Invitations
```

| K Value | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| K > 1 | Viral growth—each user produces more than one new user |
| K = 0.5 | Each user produces 0.5 new users on average |
| K < 0.1 | Referral program has minimal growth impact |

Most B2B SaaS products achieve K of 0.1–0.3. Even 0.3 meaningfully reduces blended CAC when measured over time.

### Referral Program Best Practices
- **Make the referral easy:** Pre-written email templates, one-click sharing, a unique referral link always visible
- **Time the ask correctly:** After a success milestone, not at sign-up
- **Track attribution rigorously:** Use unique referral codes tied to each advocate
- **Communicate program status:** Show referrers their pending and paid rewards
- **Test incentive types:** Cash vs credit vs product-native rewards—what resonates depends on your audience

## User-Generated Content (UGC) Strategy

UGC is any content—social posts, videos, screenshots, reviews, community answers—created by your customers about your product. It is the most authentic form of marketing.

### UGC Types and Uses

| UGC Type | Where It Lives | Best Marketing Use |
|---|---|---|
| Social media posts | Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram | Social proof, ad creative, community engagement |
| Screenshots/demos | Twitter, Reddit, community | Feature validation, onboarding content |
| Video testimonials | YouTube, LinkedIn, website | Sales enablement, landing page conversion |
| Review site content | G2, Capterra, TrustRadius | SEO, bottom-of-funnel trust signal |
| Community answers | Slack, Discord, forums | Knowledge base, content repurposing |
| Blog posts / tutorials | Customer's own blog | Co-marketing, SEO link building |

### Generating UGC Systematically
- **Create shareworthy moments:** Milestone celebrations, achievement badges, and summary reports that users want to share
- **Ask at peak happiness:** After a success milestone, in the product, with one-click sharing
- **Build the community platform:** Give UGC a home—Slack community, forum, or Discord server
- **Feature and amplify:** Repost, highlight, and reward users who create great content
- **Provide templates and prompts:** Lower the effort barrier ("Here's how to share your win")

## Case Study Production Process

Case studies are the single most effective bottom-of-funnel content asset. A prospect who reads a case study from a peer company in their industry is significantly more likely to convert.

### Case Study Production Pipeline

**Step 1 – Identify candidates**
- Customers with strong ROI metrics (time saved, revenue generated, costs reduced)
- Companies recognizable by your target market
- Champions willing to participate and get approval from their marketing team

**Step 2 – Conduct the interview**
Core case study interview questions:
- What challenge were you trying to solve before [Product]?
- What alternatives did you consider? Why did you choose us?
- How did you implement and onboard the team?
- What specific results have you achieved? (Ask for data)
- What would you tell a peer considering [Product]?

**Step 3 – Write and structure**
Classic structure: **Challenge → Solution → Results**
- Quantify results wherever possible ("reduced time-to-report by 60%," "saved $120,000 annually")
- Include a pull quote from the champion
- Add company logo, headshot, and company description for credibility

**Step 4 – Review and approve**
- Share draft with champion for accuracy
- Get legal/marketing sign-off from the customer's organization (build 2-week buffer into timeline)

**Step 5 – Distribute and repurpose**
- Website case studies page
- Sales deck reference slide
- Email nurture sequence
- Paid ad creative (stat highlight + logo)
- Social proof on landing pages

## Customer Testimonial Collection

**Testimonials are the minimum viable form of social proof.** Every company should have them; most companies treat collection as an afterthought.

### Systematic Collection Methods
- **Post-NPS survey prompt:** Ask promoters immediately after the survey with a text box for a quote
- **In-app request:** Surface a "share your experience" request at success moments
- **CSM-assisted collection:** CSMs ask directly during QBRs or milestone calls
- **Review site redirect:** Prompt users to post their testimonial to G2/Capterra for double-duty value
- **LinkedIn recommendations:** Ask champions to post a LinkedIn recommendation (visible to their network)

**Testimonial effectiveness hierarchy:**
1. **Video testimonial** (most powerful)
2. **Named quote with photo, title, and company**
3. **Named quote with title only**
4. **Anonymous quote** (least credible—avoid if possible)

## G2, Capterra, and Review Site Strategy

Review sites are where B2B buyers do pre-purchase research. Presence and ratings here directly impact conversion rates and competitive positioning.

**Review generation tactics:**
- Run **quarterly review drives:** Email your NPS promoters with a direct link and 2-sentence ask
- **In-app prompts:** Surface review requests after specific success events, not randomly
- **CSM outreach:** Incorporate review requests into the standard success motion for healthy accounts
- **Incentivize ethically:** Gift cards to advocates are acceptable; paying per positive review violates platform terms

**Review site management:**
- Respond to **every review** (positive and negative) within 7 days
- For negative reviews: acknowledge, take accountability, describe what changed
- Monitor competitor reviews for insight into their weaknesses—and your messaging opportunities
- Keep product category listings updated with accurate features and integrations

## Customer Community Building

A thriving customer community is one of the most durable competitive moats. Customers who are active in a product community have dramatically higher retention and expansion rates.

**Community platform options:**
- **Slack/Discord:** Best for technical products and developer-heavy audiences; informal, high-velocity
- **Circle/Mighty Networks:** Structured community platform; better for courses, cohorts, events
- **In-product community:** Most integrated but requires significant engineering investment

**Community growth stages:**
1. **Seed phase:** Personally invite 20–50 of your most enthusiastic customers; seed discussions manually
2. **Growth phase:** Launch events, AMAs, and user-led content; reward top contributors with recognition
3. **Maturity phase:** Members answer each other's questions; community becomes a retention and acquisition asset

## NPS as an Advocacy System

NPS should not be a metric—it should be a **workflow trigger**.

**NPS response playbook:**

| Score | Segment | Immediate Action | Follow-Up |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9–10 | Promoter | Immediate personal thank-you; referral/review/case study ask | Add to advocacy program |
| 7–8 | Passive | "How can we get to 10?" question | Feature education or success milestone push |
| 0–6 | Detractor | CSM call within 24 hours; escalate to CS leadership | Root cause resolution plan |

**NPS survey best practices:**
- Send at **behavioral triggers** (90-day mark, first major success milestone), not arbitrary calendar dates
- Follow the rating question with ONE open-text "why" question
- Analyze text responses for theme clusters—these drive product and service improvements
- Share monthly NPS trend data and verbatim quotes with product and executive teams

## Measuring Advocacy Impact on Growth

| Metric | What It Measures | How to Track |
|---|---|---|
| **Referral conversion rate** | % of referrals who become customers | UTM tracking + referral codes |
| **Referral-sourced ARR** | Revenue attributable to referral program | CRM source tracking |
| **Review volume & rating** | Brand trust on G2/Capterra | Review site dashboards |
| **Case study influenced pipeline** | Deals touched by a case study | CRM content attribution |
| **Community engagement rate** | Activity as % of members | Community platform analytics |
| **Advocacy program ROI** | Revenue generated vs program cost | Compare referral ARR to program spend |
| **Viral coefficient (K)** | Growth multiplier from referrals | (Invites sent × conversion rate) |

## Key Takeaways

- Customer advocacy is a **system, not a campaign**—it requires structured identification, activation, amplification, and reward layers
- **Double-sided referral incentives** aligned with product value outperform cash-only programs
- Case studies require a **production pipeline** with clear steps and stakeholder management—ad-hoc production never scales
- NPS is worthless as a vanity metric; it becomes powerful as a **workflow trigger** for advocacy and escalation
- Community investment compounds over years—start early and commit to the long game

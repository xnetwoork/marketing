# Part 50: Quarterly Strategy Refactor

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this part, you will be able to:
- Implement a structured quarterly strategy review process
- Identify what to examine, challenge, and update each quarter
- Reallocate budgets based on performance data and market signals
- Build an adaptive marketing organization that improves continuously
- Close the loop from measurement back to strategy

---

## Why Quarterly Strategy Refactors Are Non-Negotiable

Most marketing teams set annual plans and then execute them mechanically for 12 months, regardless of what the data, market, or competition is telling them. This is a prescription for mediocrity at best, and catastrophic resource waste at worst.

Markets move. Customer behavior shifts. Competitors launch new products. Algorithms change. What worked in Q1 may be irrelevant by Q3.

**The Quarterly Strategy Refactor** is a structured process that treats strategy as a living document — one that is continuously updated based on real-world feedback. It sits between the rigidity of annual planning and the chaos of constant pivoting. It gives your organization stability with adaptability.

---

## The Four Layers of a Quarterly Refactor

### Layer 1: Performance vs. Objectives Review

Start by honestly measuring where you are relative to where you said you would be.

**Key questions:**
- Did you hit your North Star Metric target? By how much?
- Which channels over- or under-performed?
- What was your actual CAC vs. target CAC?
- Did pipeline velocity improve or slow down?
- What was your conversion rate at each funnel stage?

**Framework: Red/Yellow/Green Scorecard**

| Metric | Target | Actual | Status | Root Cause |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|------------|
| MQLs | 500 | 420 | 🟡 | Paid social CPL increased |
| SQLs | 150 | 130 | 🟡 | Lead quality dropped |
| CAC | $450 | $520 | 🔴 | Mix shift to expensive channels |
| ROAS (Paid) | 3.5x | 4.1x | 🟢 | Creative refresh worked |
| Organic Traffic | +20% | +28% | 🟢 | SEO compound effect |

Don't just note the numbers — investigate root causes. Red metrics need action plans. Yellow metrics need monitoring. Green metrics deserve replication.

---

### Layer 2: Channel Efficiency Analysis

Every quarter, some channels get better and some get worse. Your job is to spot the shifts early.

**Questions to answer:**
- Which channel delivered the best CAC this quarter?
- Which channel's efficiency deteriorated vs. last quarter? Why?
- Are there new channels worth testing?
- Are you over-indexed in any single channel (risk concentration)?

**Channel efficiency scorecard (sample):**

| Channel | CAC Q3 | CAC Q4 | Delta | Recommendation |
|---------|--------|--------|-------|----------------|
| Meta Ads | $380 | $460 | +21% | Reduce spend; refresh creative |
| Google Search | $290 | $275 | -5% | Scale up |
| LinkedIn ABM | $720 | $680 | -6% | Maintain; test new audiences |
| Email Nurture | $120 | $115 | -4% | Optimize sequence; add steps |
| SEO | $95 | $80 | -16% | Double down; increase content budget |

Move budget toward what's working. Don't hold onto channels out of organizational inertia.

---

### Layer 3: Market & Competitive Intelligence Update

Your strategy was built on a market picture. That picture changes. Update it every quarter.

**Check:**
- **Competitor moves:** Did any competitor launch a new product, change pricing, or make a major acquisition?
- **Market signals:** Is demand growing, plateauing, or contracting? What does search trend data show?
- **Customer signals:** What are customers saying now that they weren't saying 90 days ago? Review new support tickets, reviews, and win/loss data.
- **Macro environment:** Are there economic, regulatory, or cultural shifts affecting your buyers?

**Practical tools for quarterly intel:**
- Run fresh ad library reviews of top 3 competitors
- Pull updated keyword trend data (Google Trends, SEMrush)
- Conduct 5–10 customer conversations specifically about what's changed for them
- Review G2/Capterra page and competitor reviews for new patterns

---

### Layer 4: Positioning and Messaging Refresh

Every six months, formally challenge your positioning. Every quarter, review your messaging performance.

**Messaging audit questions:**
- Which headlines, subject lines, and ad copy performed best? What makes them work?
- Are there new pain points or desired outcomes emerging that our current messaging doesn't address?
- Is our positioning still differentiated, or has the competitive landscape shifted underneath us?
- Do our top-of-funnel messages still convert at expected rates?

If conversion rates on evergreen campaigns are declining, it's often a messaging freshness problem, not a channel problem.

## The Quarterly Refactor Process — Step by Step

### Week 1 of Quarter: Data Gathering (Days 1–5)

- Pull all KPI data from dashboards and compile into a master scorecard
- Gather channel-by-channel CAC and ROAS data
- Collect qualitative inputs: sales team feedback, customer interviews summary, support ticket themes
- Run a competitive intel sweep
- Survey your marketing team for blockers and opportunities

**Output:** Quarterly Performance Report (quantitative + qualitative)

---

### Week 2: Team Debrief Sessions

- Hold a 90-minute marketing team debrief: What worked? What didn't? What surprised us?
- Hold a 60-minute sales/marketing alignment session: Are leads getting better or worse?
- Hold a 30-minute channel specialist review for each major channel (paid, SEO, email, etc.)

**Use the "Start / Stop / Continue" framework:**
- **Start:** What should we begin doing that we haven't tried?
- **Stop:** What should we stop doing that isn't working?
- **Continue:** What's working and should be accelerated?

---

### Week 3: Strategy Session & Decision Making

Bring leaders together to make decisions based on the data.

**Agenda for a 3-hour Quarterly Strategy Session:**
1. Review the Red/Yellow/Green scorecard (30 min)
2. Channel efficiency analysis and reallocation discussion (45 min)
3. Market and competitive update (30 min)
4. Positioning and messaging review (30 min)
5. Priority setting for the coming quarter (45 min)

**Decision log template:**

| Decision | Rationale | Owner | Timeline |
|----------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Reduce Meta budget by 20% | CAC increased 21%; creative fatigue | Paid Media Lead | Week 1 next Q |
| Launch LinkedIn ABM for enterprise | Pipeline gap in enterprise segment | Demand Gen Manager | Month 2 next Q |
| Refresh homepage headline | Conversion rate down 8% vs benchmark | CRO Lead | Week 2 next Q |

---

### Week 4: Plan Activation

- Update your 90-day plan with revised priorities and resource allocation
- Brief channels owners on changes
- Update dashboards and KPI targets for the new quarter
- Communicate changes cross-functionally (sales, product, leadership)

---

## Budget Reallocation Framework

Use this decision tree for budget moves:

1. **Channel is performing (CAC ≤ target, scaling efficiently)** → Scale up 20–30%
2. **Channel is underperforming but fixable** → Give it one more quarter with a specific hypothesis to test
3. **Channel is underperforming and has no clear fix** → Reduce or pause and reallocate
4. **New channel has shown early promise in tests** → Graduate to a larger test budget
5. **Macro environment has shifted** → Reassess the entire portfolio mix

**Never reallocate based on one bad week.** Look at 4–8 week trends before making significant moves. But also don't wait 6 months if the signal is clear.

---

## Rolling 12-Month Planning

Instead of an annual plan that becomes stale, maintain a rolling 12-month view:

- **Month 1–3 (current quarter):** Locked execution plan with specific tactics
- **Month 4–6 (next quarter):** Draft plan with confirmed priorities
- **Month 7–12:** Strategic direction with major milestones, not detailed tactics

At each quarterly refactor, you extend the rolling horizon by one quarter and lock in the next 90 days. This balances stability with adaptability.

## Building an Adaptive Marketing Organization

The Quarterly Refactor is only possible if your organization is built for adaptation.

**Organizational characteristics of adaptive marketing teams:**
- **Data literacy:** Every marketer can read and interpret their own metrics without relying on analysts for basic insights
- **Psychological safety:** Team members surface bad news early, not hide it until the quarterly review
- **Decision speed:** Budget reallocation decisions can be made in days, not months
- **Experiment mindset:** Failure of a hypothesis is treated as learning, not failure of a person
- **Customer obsession:** Regular customer conversations are a standard part of every marketer's calendar

---

## The Compound Effect of Quarterly Refactors

Teams that consistently run quarterly refactors outperform those that don't — not because any single refactor is a breakthrough, but because of **compounding incremental improvement**.

- Each quarter, you remove 1–2 low-performing tactics and replace them with better ones
- Each quarter, your understanding of your customers deepens
- Each quarter, your messaging gets sharper
- Each quarter, your channel mix gets more efficient

Over 4 quarters, this compounds into a dramatically more effective marketing operation — with lower CAC, higher conversion rates, better retention, and a team that knows exactly what levers to pull.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Strategy is a living document** — update it every 90 days based on real-world data
- **Four layers:** performance vs. objectives, channel efficiency, market/competitive intel, messaging refresh
- **Process matters:** data gathering → team debrief → strategy session → plan activation
- **Move budget to winners** — every quarter, improve your channel mix based on CAC and efficiency data
- **Rolling 12-month planning** balances short-term execution with long-term direction
- **Adaptive organizations** build the culture and processes to act on what quarterly reviews reveal

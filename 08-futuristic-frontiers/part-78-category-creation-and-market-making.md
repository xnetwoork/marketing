## The Theory of Category Creation

Most marketing strategy focuses on how to win within existing categories: capture market share, outbid competitors, improve product-market fit on established dimensions. **Category creation** is a fundamentally different strategic game. It asks: *What if we define the problem space so that our solution is the only logical answer?*

The foundational intellectual framework comes from the **Play Bigger** model (Al Ramadan, Dave Peterson, Christopher Lochhead, Kevin Maney — *Play Bigger*, 2016), which argues:

> In technology markets, **the category king captures approximately 76% of the total economic value** created in that category — including market cap, revenue, and talent. Being second-best in a well-defined category is far less valuable than being first in a category you define.

This is not hyperbole — it reflects how **human cognition organizes markets**. Buyers need mental categories to evaluate vendors. When a new category is successfully established, the company that designed it occupies the "king" position in the buyer's mental model before competitors even understand what game is being played.

---

## Why Category Kings Capture Disproportionate Value

The winner-take-most dynamic in categories emerges from several reinforcing mechanisms:

- **Cognitive anchoring**: The first brand associated with a new category becomes the reference point — all subsequent vendors are evaluated relative to the pioneer *(Salesforce = cloud CRM, HubSpot = inbound marketing, Gong = revenue intelligence)*
- **Analyst legitimization**: Gartner, Forrester, and IDC create Magic Quadrants and Wave reports for named categories. The company that names the category often becomes the leader in the first quadrant
- **Ecosystem lock-in**: Partners, integrations, and complementary tools cluster around the category leader, creating switching costs for buyers
- **Talent magnetism**: Top professionals want to work in the fastest-growing category, not a commodity segment
- **Media narrative**: Journalists need categories to write about. Category creators receive disproportionate coverage

| Category | Creator | Captured Value |
|---|---|---|
| Cloud CRM | Salesforce | >$200B market cap at peak |
| Inbound Marketing | HubSpot | Named and owned the term; IPO'd on category narrative |
| Revenue Intelligence | Gong | Defined the space before competitors realized it existed |
| Conversational Marketing | Drift | Renamed real-time website chat as a demand gen strategy |
| Zero Trust Security | Palo Alto / Forrester | Redefined network security architecture |

## The Category Design Process

Category design is not an accident. It is a deliberate, multi-stage process:

### Step 1: Identify a Problem No One Has Named

The starting point is always a **problem in the world** — not a product feature. The problem must be:
- **Real**: Buyers experience it, even if they cannot name it
- **Unresolved**: No existing category solution adequately addresses it
- **Large**: Significant enough to justify a new market
- **Nameable**: Capable of being articulated in a phrase that creates an "aha" moment

*HubSpot identified that outbound interruption-based marketing was losing effectiveness as consumers gained control — a real, unresolved, large problem they named "the inbound marketing problem."*

### Step 2: Name the Problem and the Category

The **category name** does two things simultaneously: it names the problem (establishing urgency) and implies your product category as the solution. Effective category names are:
- Simple and memorable (1–3 words)
- Descriptive of the new behavior or outcome, not the technology
- Ownable (not generic industry terms that competitors can claim equally)

### Step 3: Design the Category Narrative

The **category narrative** (also called the Point of View or POV document) is the core intellectual property of category creation. It articulates:
- The old world (how buyers have been operating)
- The problem with the old world (why it is failing them)
- The new world (what becomes possible when the category problem is solved)
- The implications for the buyer (why they must act now)

The POV is not a product pitch. It is a **worldview document** that positions the category creator as a thought leader, not just a vendor.

### Step 4: Execute Lightning Strikes and Mobilization Events

A **lightning strike** is a concentrated, high-impact event or moment designed to force the category into the market's consciousness simultaneously. Examples:
- A landmark research report ("The State of Revenue Intelligence")
- A live conference designed around the category (HubSpot's INBOUND, Drift's HYPERGROWTH, Gong's Celebrate)
- A viral manifesto or book (*Play Bigger* itself, *They Ask You Answer*)
- An analyst briefing campaign timed to generate a new category designation

> **Research Gap**
>
> Category creation is documented almost exclusively through retrospective case studies of companies that succeeded — Salesforce, HubSpot, Drift, Gong, Salesforce, Intercom. The **base rate** of category creation attempts — including the vast majority that failed or produced no meaningful market position — has not been systematically studied. We do not know how many companies attempt deliberate category creation per year, what percentage successfully establish the category, what distinguishes successful from failed attempts, or how long category establishment typically takes. The field currently operates on survivorship-biased success stories.

## POV Documentation and Thought Leadership as Category Tools

The POV document is both an internal strategic artifact and an external marketing weapon. High-leverage POV distribution channels:

- **A manifesto or book** — crystallizes the category narrative in a form that spreads independently *(HubSpot's "Inbound Marketing" book, Drift's "Conversational Marketing")*
- **Analyst briefings** — systematically brief Gartner, Forrester, IDC, and G2 on the new category framework before competitors do
- **CEO/founder keynotes** at industry events — leadership visibility in the category narrative establishes personal authority
- **Executive ghostwriting and LinkedIn thought leadership** — distribute POV fragments to key buyer personas at scale
- **Commissioned research** — fund studies that quantify the problem your category solves, creating independently validated urgency

## Analyst and Media Relations in Category Creation

Analysts and journalists are **category legitimizers**. A new category does not exist until respected third parties are writing about it.

**Analyst strategy for category creation:**
1. Brief the top 3 analysts in adjacent categories 12–18 months before your planned category launch
2. Provide proprietary data that supports the category narrative (market size estimates, customer ROI data)
3. Advocate for a new category or subcategory designation (not just a position within an existing one)
4. Provide lighthouse customer references who will speak to analysts independently

**Media strategy:**
- Target **trade publications** that cover your buyer's function (not just tech press)
- Pitch the **problem story**, not the product story — journalists cover problems, not vendor features
- Build a **media advisory board** of influential voices who can amplify the category narrative

## When to Create a Category vs. Compete in an Existing One

Category creation is not always the right strategy. The decision framework:

| Condition | Create a Category | Compete in Existing Category |
|---|---|---|
| Problem is genuinely unnamed | ✓ | |
| Existing category solutions are fundamentally inadequate | ✓ | |
| You have a 3–5 year runway before well-funded competitors follow | ✓ | |
| Existing category has clear leader with strong moat | | ✓ |
| Buyer vocabulary already exists and is widely used | | ✓ |
| Your product is differentiated on dimension buyers already value | | ✓ |
| Capital and time constraints limit category marketing investment | | ✓ |

## Risks and Failure Modes of Category Creation

- **Too early**: Category narrative resonates but market isn't ready — company runs out of capital before category achieves mass
- **Too abstract**: POV is intellectually compelling but doesn't translate to clear buyer action
- **Competitor co-option**: Competitors adopt the category name, diluting the creator's ownership
- **Analyst resistance**: Major analysts do not adopt the category framing, preventing legitimization
- **Product-narrative mismatch**: Category narrative promises capabilities the product cannot yet deliver
- **Internal misalignment**: Sales team reverts to feature-selling rather than category-selling, undermining the narrative

## Case Studies in Brief

- **Salesforce (Cloud CRM)**: Named the problem "no software" and launched at a competitor's conference with protesters wearing "No Software" t-shirts — the original lightning strike
- **HubSpot (Inbound Marketing)**: Co-authored a book, built a conference (INBOUND), created a certification program, and commissioned annual research — systematic category infrastructure over a decade
- **Drift (Conversational Marketing)**: Declared email dead, wrote a manifesto, hosted HYPERGROWTH conference, partnered with David Cancel as the visible category evangelist
- **Gong (Revenue Intelligence)**: Funded the "Revenue Intelligence" category with proprietary research from millions of recorded sales calls — data-driven category creation

## Frontier Signals

- **"Category Pirates" newsletter** (Lochhead, Peterson, Redmond) has become the leading ongoing practitioner community for category design — signal that category creation is professionalizing as a discipline
- **Bevy and community-led category creation**: New playbook where community (not conference) becomes the primary category mobilization vehicle
- **AI-generated category signals**: Startups like Warmly and Clay are attempting to define "AI-native GTM" as a category — watching whether AI tool categories consolidate or fracture
- **Gartner's pace of new category creation**: Gartner added 14 new Magic Quadrant categories in 2023 — indicating accelerating market fragmentation that creates category creation opportunities
- **Paper: "Market Creation and the Distribution of Power in Platform Ecosystems" (Strategic Management Journal, 2022)** provides adjacent academic framework for understanding category economic dynamics

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Category kings capture ~76% of category economics** — the strategic return on successful category creation is enormous
- **Category design is a process**: name the problem, design the narrative, execute lightning strikes, mobilize ecosystem
- **The POV document** is the core intellectual property of category creation — not the product roadmap
- **Analysts and media are legitimizers** — systematic relationship investment is required before a category launch, not after
- **Create categories when the problem is genuinely unnamed and your solution is structurally differentiated** — competing in an existing category is often the smarter play
- **Survivorship bias** in category creation case studies demands critical evaluation — the failure rate is unknown but likely high

# Part 26: Social Media Strategy

## Learning Objectives
- Understand how to develop a social media strategy tied to business goals
- Learn how different platforms serve different strategic purposes
- Build a sustainable social media operation that drives real results

---

## Social Media as a Strategic Tool

Social media is often treated as a tactical afterthought — something the marketing intern handles or something you do because "everyone's on social." This approach produces mediocre results and burnout.

Effective social media marketing starts with strategy:
- **What business outcomes should social media drive?** (Brand awareness, lead generation, community, customer retention?)
- **Who is the audience?** (Different platforms, different demographics, different content expectations)
- **What is the right role for social in the marketing mix?** (Not every business needs every platform)
- **What can we sustain consistently at quality?** (Better to be excellent on one platform than mediocre on five)

## Platform Strategy: Choosing Where to Play

**Facebook/Meta**
- Demographic: Broadest reach, particularly 30-55+ in many markets
- Content that works: Video (especially Facebook Watch), community groups, events
- Strength for marketers: Unmatched paid targeting capability; large communities
- Organic reach: Significantly declined; primarily a paid platform for brands

**Instagram**
- Demographic: Strong 18-35, visual-first audience
- Content that works: High-quality visuals, Reels (short-form video), Stories, carousels
- Strength: Visual brand building, DTC commerce (Instagram Shopping), influencer partnerships
- Organic reach: Moderate and algorithm-dependent; Reels given preference

**LinkedIn**
- Demographic: Professional B2B audience; decision-makers and practitioners
- Content that works: Professional insights, thought leadership, career stories, industry analysis
- Strength: Unparalleled for B2B; organic reach still relatively strong for quality content
- Best for: B2B brands, professional services, talent attraction

**YouTube**
- Demographic: Second largest search engine; broad demographics
- Content that works: In-depth tutorials, product demos, documentaries, vlogs
- Strength: Long-form video with high SEO value; evergreen content compounds over time
- Investment: High production relative to other platforms; ROI is long-term

**TikTok**
- Demographic: Dominant in 18-34 (growing 35+); algorithmic discovery
- Content that works: Entertainment-first, authentic/unpolished, trend-driven, storytelling
- Strength: Extraordinary organic reach through algorithm; viral potential
- Challenge: Fast content cadence; trend relevance fades quickly

**X (Twitter)**
- Demographic: Media professionals, tech sector, political discourse
- Content that works: Real-time commentary, short insights, threads
- Strength: Thought leadership for specific industries (VC, tech, media, politics)
- Organic reach: Declined significantly for brands; best for individuals and real-time moments

**Pinterest**
- Demographic: Predominantly female, DIY/lifestyle/home/fashion
- Content that works: Inspiring imagery, how-to content, recipes, products
- Strength: Purchase intent; evergreen discovery; strong for e-commerce in relevant categories

## The Social Media Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Define Platform Goals

Different platforms serve different marketing objectives. Be explicit:

| Platform | Primary Goal |
|----------|-------------|
| Instagram | Brand awareness, visual brand building |
| LinkedIn | B2B lead generation, thought leadership |
| YouTube | SEO, product education, retention |
| Email (+ social) | Conversion and nurturing |

### Step 2: Define Your Content Mix

A useful model: the **4-1-1 rule** or similar ratio:
- 4 parts educational/entertaining value-add content
- 1 part curated content from others
- 1 part brand/promotional content

Or the **Rule of Thirds:**
- 1/3 brand promotion
- 1/3 personal/humanizing content
- 1/3 community engagement and curation

The exact ratio matters less than the principle: *most content should add value, not just ask for something.*

### Step 3: Build Platform-Specific Content Plans

Content that works on LinkedIn (professional analysis, career stories) often fails on TikTok (entertainment-first, relatable moments). Don't repurpose without adaptation.

For each priority platform:
- What content formats does the algorithm favor?
- What does the audience expect and engage with?
- What cadence is required to maintain momentum?
- What does "great" look like for this platform?

### Step 4: Define Your Engagement Strategy

Social media is not a broadcast medium. Engagement — responding to comments, joining conversations, following relevant accounts — signals to algorithms and builds community.

Set a daily engagement practice: 15-30 minutes responding to comments, engaging with relevant posts in your space, and building genuine relationships.

## Organic vs. Paid Social

**Organic social** builds community, creates brand voice, and nurtures existing audiences. It's slow to scale but creates authentic engagement.

**Paid social** (Facebook Ads, LinkedIn Ads, TikTok Ads) delivers targeted reach at scale, quickly. Essential for:
- Reaching new audiences who don't know you
- Promoting specific campaigns or offers
- Retargeting website visitors
- Lead generation

The two should work together: paid social drives cold audiences to your profile/content, organic social converts and retains them.

## Social Media Metrics That Matter

**Awareness metrics:** Reach, impressions, follower growth
**Engagement metrics:** Engagement rate (likes + comments + shares / reach), saves, comments per post
**Traffic metrics:** Click-through rate (CTR), website sessions from social
**Conversion metrics:** Leads, sign-ups, purchases attributed to social
**Community metrics:** Brand mentions, sentiment, DM volume

Vanity metrics (follower count, total likes) are less meaningful than engagement rate and downstream conversion.

## Building a Sustainable Social Operation

Social media burnout is real. Build sustainability through:
- **Batching:** Create a week or month of content in one session
- **Templates:** Repeatable content formats that require less creative effort
- **Repurposing:** Turn one piece of long-form content into multiple social posts
- **Scheduling:** Use tools (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite, Sprout) to schedule in advance
- **Content calendar:** See [Part 22](../content/part-22-content-calendars.md)

---

## Key Takeaways

- Social media strategy must start with business goals and audience definition, not platform defaults
- Choose 1-2 platforms to do exceptionally well rather than 5 platforms poorly
- Different platforms serve different strategic purposes; adapt content and objectives accordingly
- Most social content should add value; promotional content should be the exception
- Combine organic social (community and authenticity) with paid social (reach and targeting) for maximum impact

# Part 27: Paid Advertising (PPC/SEM)

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the fundamentals of pay-per-click and search engine marketing
- Learn how to structure, launch, and optimize paid advertising campaigns
- Develop a framework for evaluating whether paid advertising makes sense for your business

---

## What Is Paid Advertising?

Paid advertising (also called PPC — Pay Per Click — or SEM — Search Engine Marketing) is any digital advertising model where you pay to display your message to a target audience, typically paying per click, impression, or conversion.

Unlike organic channels (SEO, social, content), paid advertising can drive traffic and leads immediately. The tradeoff: it stops working the moment you stop paying. The strategic question is always: is the cost of acquiring a customer through paid advertising less than the lifetime value that customer generates?

---

## The Main Paid Advertising Channels

### Search Advertising (Google/Bing Ads)
Your ad appears in search results when someone searches for a relevant keyword.

**Why it's powerful:** The highest commercial intent of any advertising channel. A person searching "project management software for teams" is in active evaluation mode.

**How it works:**
- You bid on keywords (the maximum you'll pay per click)
- Google's auction determines ad position (bid × quality score)
- Quality Score is based on: expected CTR, ad relevance to keyword, landing page quality
- You pay only when someone clicks

**Best for:** Direct response; capturing existing demand; high-intent queries

### Display Advertising (Google Display Network, programmatic)
Visual banner and image ads that appear on websites across the internet.

**Why it's powerful:** Massive reach; visual brand building; powerful for retargeting

**How it works:**
- Target by audience demographics, interests, keywords, or placement
- Typically CPM (cost per thousand impressions) or CPC pricing
- Creative must work in small formats and capture attention quickly

**Best for:** Brand awareness; retargeting website visitors; creating demand before people search

### Paid Social Advertising
Promoted content and ads on social platforms (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, Pinterest, X).

Each platform covered in detail in [Part 26](./part-26-social-media-strategy.md). Key distinctions:
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram):** Best targeting for B2C by demographics, interests, and behavior
- **LinkedIn Ads:** Unmatched B2B targeting by job title, company, industry, seniority
- **TikTok Ads:** Emerging platform with low CPMs and high engagement for right audiences
- **Pinterest Ads:** Strong for consumer purchase-intent audiences

## Setting Up for Success: Campaign Structure

### Google Search: Fundamental Structure

**Account → Campaigns → Ad Groups → Ads + Keywords**

- **Campaign:** Sets budget, bidding strategy, and targeting (geography, device)
- **Ad Group:** Groups closely related keywords with relevant ads
- **Keywords:** The search terms that trigger your ads
- **Ads:** The creative that appears; at least 3 responsive search ads per ad group recommended

### Match Types (Google)
- **Broad match:** Widest reach; Google interprets related queries
- **Phrase match:** Ads show for queries containing your keyword phrase
- **Exact match:** Ads show only for exact query (or very close variants)

Start with phrase and exact match; broaden as you learn what performs.

### Negative Keywords
Critically important: terms for which you *don't* want to show ads. Prevents irrelevant clicks that waste budget.
- Example: A B2B software company might add "free," "crack," "torrent" as negatives

## Targeting: The Foundation of Paid Social

The power of paid social is targeting precision. On Meta:

**Core audiences:**
- Demographics (age, gender, location, education)
- Interests (what pages they follow, activities)
- Behaviors (purchase behavior, travel, device use)

**Custom audiences:**
- Upload your customer list to find them on Facebook
- Website visitors (requires Facebook Pixel installation)
- Video viewers, page engagers, app users

**Lookalike audiences:**
- Find new people who share characteristics with your best customers
- Start with 1-2% lookalikes for quality; expand to 5-10% for scale

## The Campaign Launch Checklist

Before spending, verify:
- [ ] Conversion tracking is installed and firing correctly
- [ ] Landing page is optimized for the offer (see [Part 32](./part-32-conversion-rate-optimization.md))
- [ ] Budget is appropriate for learning (Google needs ~50 conversions/month to optimize)
- [ ] Negative keywords are populated (search campaigns)
- [ ] Multiple ad variations are live for testing
- [ ] UTM parameters are in all URLs for analytics tracking
- [ ] Target audience is defined and segment size is sufficient

---

## Optimization: The Ongoing Process

Paid advertising requires continuous optimization:

**Weekly:**
- Review spend vs. budget
- Check impression share and auction insights
- Pause underperforming ads and keywords
- Add new negative keywords from search term reports

**Monthly:**
- Review campaign performance vs. goals
- Test new ad copy variations
- Adjust bids based on performance by device, time, location
- Analyze audience performance in paid social; expand or contract

**Quarterly:**
- Strategic review: is paid advertising generating positive ROI?
- Audit account structure
- Test new campaign types or audiences
- Review competitive landscape

## Key Metrics for Paid Advertising

| Metric | Definition | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Impressions** | Times your ad was shown | Reach measurement |
| **CTR** | Clicks ÷ Impressions | Ad relevance and appeal |
| **CPC** | Cost ÷ Clicks | Efficiency of traffic buying |
| **CPL** | Cost ÷ Leads | Efficiency of lead acquisition |
| **CPA** | Cost ÷ Conversions (customers) | Efficiency of customer acquisition |
| **ROAS** | Revenue ÷ Ad Spend | Direct revenue return |
| **LTV:CAC ratio** | Customer lifetime value ÷ acquisition cost | Long-term profitability |

Optimize toward the metric that's closest to business value. CTR optimization without conversion optimization can lead you astray.

---

## The Paid Advertising Learning Budget

New campaigns need data to optimize. Under-funded campaigns don't generate enough conversions for algorithmic optimization to work.

**Rule of thumb:** Budget at least 10x your target CPA per month. If your target CPA is $50, budget at least $500/month per campaign to gather enough data to optimize.

Many advertisers kill campaigns too early when they're just beginning to learn. Give campaigns 4-6 weeks before making major structural changes.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Paid advertising delivers immediate, scalable reach — but requires ongoing investment and optimization
- Search advertising captures existing demand; social advertising creates demand
- Campaign structure, targeting, and conversion tracking must be set up correctly before spending
- Optimization is continuous: weekly tactical, monthly performance review, quarterly strategic
- Success requires healthy LTV:CAC ratios — paid advertising only works when customers are worth more than they cost to acquire

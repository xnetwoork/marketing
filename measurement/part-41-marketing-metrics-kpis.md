# Part 41: Marketing Metrics & KPIs

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the hierarchy of marketing metrics and which ones actually matter
- Learn the most important KPIs at each stage of the customer lifecycle
- Build a metrics framework that drives better decisions rather than just more reporting

---

## The Problem with Marketing Metrics

Marketing can measure almost everything today. Click rates, impressions, reach, engagement, open rates, bounce rates, session duration, follower counts — the data is endless. The curse of abundance: more data doesn't automatically produce better decisions.

Two failure modes:
1. **Measuring the wrong things** — optimizing for vanity metrics that don't connect to business outcomes
2. **Measuring too many things** — so many metrics that attention is diluted and nothing is acted on

The solution is a clear metrics hierarchy: a small number of metrics that directly measure business outcomes, supported by a larger set of diagnostic metrics that explain *why* those outcomes are moving.

---

## The Metrics Hierarchy

### Tier 1: Business Outcome Metrics
These directly measure business value. If marketing activity doesn't ultimately affect these, it's not creating business value.

- **Revenue attributed to marketing** — what dollar amount of sales can marketing claim credit for?
- **New customers acquired** — how many new customers did marketing generate this period?
- **Customer lifetime value (CLV)** — are we acquiring high-value customers?
- **Marketing ROI** — what return does the business get on marketing investment?

### Tier 2: Leading Indicators
These predict future business outcomes and are actionable in the medium term.

- **Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs)** — volume of qualified leads passed to sales
- **MQL-to-SQL conversion rate** — quality of leads generated
- **Trial activations or free signups** — top of product funnel
- **Email list growth rate** — building the owned audience
- **Organic search traffic** — long-term demand generation asset

### Tier 3: Channel and Campaign Metrics
These diagnose performance within specific channels and tactics.

- **Email:** Open rate, click-through rate, unsubscribe rate, conversion rate
- **SEO:** Organic traffic, keyword rankings, backlinks, click-through rate from search
- **Paid advertising:** Impressions, CTR, CPC, CPL, CPA, ROAS
- **Social:** Reach, engagement rate, follower growth, referral traffic
- **Content:** Pageviews, time on page, downloads, conversion rate of gated content

## Essential KPIs by Business Model

### SaaS/Subscription Business

| KPI | Definition | Healthy Benchmark |
|-----|-----------|------------------|
| MRR Growth Rate | Month-over-month MRR change | 10-20% for early stage |
| CAC | Total marketing + sales cost ÷ new customers | Varies by segment |
| LTV:CAC | CLV ÷ CAC | >3:1 |
| Churn Rate | Churned customers ÷ starting customers | <2% monthly for SMB SaaS |
| Net Revenue Retention | (Starting + expansion - churn) ÷ starting MRR | >100% is healthy |
| Activation Rate | % of signups who complete key setup | >60% is typically healthy |
| Time to First Value | Days from signup to first success moment | Minimize; varies by product |

### E-Commerce

| KPI | Definition | Healthy Benchmark |
|-----|-----------|------------------|
| Revenue from marketing | Direct attribution | Varies |
| ROAS | Revenue ÷ ad spend | >2-4x depending on margins |
| Email Revenue % | Revenue attributed to email | 20-30% of total revenue |
| Cart Abandonment Rate | Abandoned ÷ initiated carts | Industry average ~70%; minimize |
| Customer Repeat Rate | % of customers who purchase again | >30% is healthy |
| Average Order Value (AOV) | Revenue ÷ number of orders | Maximize through bundles, upsell |

### B2B Lead Generation

| KPI | Definition | Healthy Benchmark |
|-----|-----------|------------------|
| Cost per MQL | Marketing spend ÷ MQLs generated | Varies; track trend |
| MQL-to-SQL Rate | SQLs ÷ MQLs | 20-30% is typical |
| Sales Cycle Length | Days from MQL to close | Minimize; monitor trends |
| Win Rate | Closed-won ÷ total opportunities | Varies by industry |
| Pipeline Velocity | Pipeline value × win rate ÷ sales cycle | Maximize |
| Marketing Influenced Pipeline | Pipeline value touched by marketing | Goal: 80%+ |

## North Star Metric

Many organizations benefit from identifying a single "North Star Metric" — one number that best captures the value they deliver to customers and predicts long-term business success.

**Examples:**
- **Airbnb:** Nights booked
- **Spotify:** Monthly active listeners
- **HubSpot:** Number of marketing-qualified contacts generated for customers
- **Slack:** Messages sent

The North Star Metric aligns the entire organization around a single measure that correlates with both customer value and business success.

---

## Vanity Metrics to Watch Out For

These metrics feel good but often don't indicate real business progress:

| Vanity Metric | Why It's Misleading | What to Track Instead |
|---------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Total website visits | Raw traffic without quality context | Qualified traffic; conversion rate |
| Social media followers | Reach without engagement | Engagement rate; traffic from social |
| Email list size | Volume without engagement | Active subscribers; CTOR |
| Total app downloads | Installs without usage | Active users; activation rate |
| Press mentions | Coverage without conversion | Brand search volume; traffic from PR |

## Building Your KPI Dashboard

Choose 5-7 KPIs that:
1. Directly connect to business outcomes
2. Are within marketing's sphere of influence
3. Can be measured reliably and consistently
4. Are actionable — when they move, you know what to do

Review weekly at operational level; monthly against goals; quarterly for strategic alignment.

---

## Key Takeaways

- A metrics hierarchy organizes KPIs by business impact: outcomes → leading indicators → channel metrics
- Vanity metrics are seductive but don't indicate real business progress; tie metrics to outcomes
- Choose a small set of KPIs that are actionable and directly connected to business value
- The North Star Metric aligns teams around the single measure that best predicts long-term success
- Different business models require different KPI frameworks; adapt to your model

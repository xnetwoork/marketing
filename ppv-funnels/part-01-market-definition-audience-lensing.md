# Part 01: Market Definition & Audience Lensing

## Learning Objectives
- Define the PPV category you compete in and the boundaries that shape demand
- Segment audiences by intent, device behavior, and purchase context
- Build a market lens that guides every downstream funnel decision

---

## Define the PPV Category With Precision

PPV platforms compete in distinct micro-markets: live sports, entertainment events, creator-led streams, conferences, or education. Each category has different expectations on timing, pricing tolerance, and device usage. If your category definition is fuzzy, your funnel will attract the wrong traffic and produce inconsistent conversion rates.

**Category clarity checklist:**
- What is the dominant purchase trigger: entertainment, urgency, or professional value?
- Is the PPV event an isolated moment or part of a recurring series?
- What is the realistic alternative: free streams, on-demand libraries, or pirated content?

Write a one-sentence category statement: “We sell **live, time-bound access** to [event type] for [audience segment] who want [primary outcome].”

---

## Audience Lensing: The 4-Layer Model

Most PPV funnels fail because they treat the audience as one group. Use layered lensing to isolate segments with distinct behavior patterns.

1. **Intent Layer** — “Why now?”
   - Casual curiosity
   - Planned viewers
   - Urgent last-minute buyers
2. **Format Layer** — “How do they experience?”
   - Live-only viewers
   - Replay-first viewers
   - Social co-viewers (watch parties)
3. **Device Layer** — “Where do they convert?”
   - Mobile-first buyers
   - Desktop planners
   - TV-first viewers who need a second device for checkout
4. **Payment Layer** — “How do they pay?”
   - One-time PPV purchasers
   - Subscription upgraders
   - Gift buyers

Each layer changes the offer, messaging, and friction points you must address.

---

## Market Signals That Matter in PPV

PPV markets are highly sensitive to timing and buzz. Track signals weekly:
- **Search demand spikes** around talent announcements and trailers
- **Social volume velocity** (mentions per hour, not total mentions)
- **Press coverage cycles** (announcement → preview → recap)
- **Piracy chatter** (signals price sensitivity and tech expectations)

Use these to set your funnel intensity and ad spend windows. PPV success is not about steady-state acquisition; it’s about the right spikes at the right moments.

---

## The PPV Market Lens Canvas (Deliverable)

Create a one-page canvas that becomes the input for all later parts:
- Category statement
- Primary and secondary audience lenses
- Top 3 alternative choices customers have
- Event timing and demand spike window
- Primary conversion device

This document becomes your control panel for testing and optimization. If your funnel underperforms, revisit the lens before changing tactics.

---

## Key Takeaways
- PPV markets demand precise category definition because timing and intent dominate behavior
- Audience lensing uncovers conversion friction that broad segmentation misses
- Market signals are time-sensitive and should directly control funnel intensity
- A one-page market lens canvas prevents downstream strategy drift

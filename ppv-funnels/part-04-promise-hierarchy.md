# Part 04: Promise Hierarchy

## Learning Objectives
- Build a layered promise system that increases conversion without overclaiming
- Distinguish primary, secondary, and tertiary promises for PPV offers
- Use promise hierarchy to reduce buyer anxiety and post-purchase regret

---

## The Role of a Promise Hierarchy

A PPV purchase is a fast, emotional decision. Buyers want to know **what they get**, **why it matters**, and **what happens if something goes wrong**. A promise hierarchy arranges these answers in an order that feels natural and persuasive.

If you present all benefits at once, the message becomes noisy. If you focus only on the headline promise, buyers hesitate because they can’t verify value or manage risk.

---

## The Three Promise Layers

1. **Primary Promise (the core reason to buy)**
   - Example: “Watch the championship live.”
2. **Secondary Promise (experience enhancer)**
   - Example: “Multi-angle views, live commentary, and VIP chat.”
3. **Tertiary Promise (risk reducer)**
   - Example: “Replay available for 72 hours. Full refund if stream fails.”

Each layer answers a different buyer question: **Why? How good? What if?**

---

## How to Sequence Promises in the Funnel

- **Landing hero:** Only the primary promise + CTA
- **Mid-page:** Secondary promises with proof assets
- **Checkout:** Tertiary promises and guarantees
- **Post-purchase:** Reinforce all three in the confirmation email

The sequence prevents overselling early and keeps clarity when it matters most.

---

## Promise Integrity Checklist

Before shipping a funnel, verify:
- Can the platform deliver every promise at peak load?
- Do you have proof for the secondary promise (testimonials, demos, screenshots)?
- Are tertiary promises written in clear, enforceable language?

A broken promise damages long-term brand equity more than a lost sale.

---

## Key Takeaways
- A promise hierarchy organizes benefits into a persuasive, low-friction sequence
- Primary promise drives action; secondary promise adds desirability; tertiary promise reduces risk
- Promise integrity is a technical and brand requirement, not just marketing copy
- Consistent promise sequencing reduces confusion and checkout drop-off

# Part 05: Offer Architecture

## Learning Objectives
- Design a tiered PPV offer system that maximizes revenue without harming conversion
- Structure entry, core, and premium offers to match audience intent
- Use architectural constraints to simplify the purchase decision

---

## Why Offer Architecture Matters in PPV

PPV funnels often fail by offering either too many choices or too few. A single offer can leave money on the table, while an overcomplicated menu creates decision paralysis. Offer architecture is the disciplined structure that balances simplicity with revenue capture.

---

## The 3-Tier PPV Offer Model

1. **Core Offer (default choice)**
   - Live access + standard viewing experience
2. **Premium Offer (value stack)**
   - Live + replay + bonus content + premium chat or angles
3. **Entry Offer (lower-friction)**
   - Limited access (early rounds only, highlights, or delayed stream)

In most cases, the premium offer should be 25–60% higher than core, with clear, concrete upgrades.

---

## The Decision Simplification Rules

- **Rule 1: One primary CTA** — Make the core offer the default selection.
- **Rule 2: Distinct upgrade benefits** — Premium needs unique outcomes, not just “more.”
- **Rule 3: Obvious tradeoffs** — Entry offer must be constrained so it doesn’t cannibalize core.

If the tradeoff isn’t visible in 5 seconds, you’ve built a confusing offer set.

---

## Offer Architecture for Different PPV Types

- **Sports:** Premium = multi-angle + stats overlay + replay
- **Concerts:** Premium = backstage feed + exclusive setlist access
- **Conferences:** Premium = replays + session downloads + Q&A access
- **Creator events:** Premium = meet-and-greet or post-show stream

Architectures should match the outcomes your market values most.

---

## Key Takeaways
- PPV offers should be tiered but constrained to avoid paralysis
- The core offer must be default and easiest to understand
- Premium offers need exclusive outcomes, not just added features
- Entry offers must be clearly limited to prevent revenue leakage

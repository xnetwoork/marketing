# Part 06: Temporal Scarcity Design

## Learning Objectives
- Build scarcity mechanisms that increase urgency without eroding trust
- Align scarcity windows to real PPV event milestones
- Prevent last-minute bottlenecks and buyer regret

---

## Scarcity Is Native to PPV — Use It Correctly

PPV already has a built-in scarcity: the event happens once. Your job is to **structure that scarcity** so it drives action at the right moments without feeling manipulative. Artificial scarcity (fake timers, “limited seats” for streams) damages credibility and reduces long-term conversion.

---

## The Three Scarcity Windows

1. **Early-Bird Window**
   - Reward planners and lock in revenue early
   - Use a real price increase date
2. **Event Countdown Window**
   - Emphasize time remaining and access certainty
   - Focus on live experience value
3. **Replay Expiration Window**
   - If replay access expires, make it explicit and real

Each window should have its own messaging and CTA emphasis.

---

## Designing Scarcity Without Friction

- Avoid scarcity language that implies instability (“limited bandwidth”).
- Use calendar sync links and reminders so urgency feels helpful, not pushy.
- Keep scarcity terms consistent across ads, landing pages, and checkout.

**Trust test:** If the scarcity claim is wrong or flexible, remove it.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Scarcity works best when it reflects the real event timeline
- Separate early-bird, event countdown, and replay windows with distinct messaging
- Fake scarcity reduces conversion and raises refund risk
- Consistency across funnel stages preserves trust

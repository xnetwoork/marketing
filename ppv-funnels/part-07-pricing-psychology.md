# Part 07: Pricing Psychology

## Learning Objectives
- Use pricing cues that match PPV buyer expectations and reduce hesitation
- Apply anchoring, decoy, and framing techniques without harming trust
- Align price perception with event value and exclusivity

---

## Price in PPV Is a Signal

For PPV, the price is not just a number — it signals exclusivity, production value, and legitimacy. Underpricing can reduce perceived quality; overpricing can push viewers toward free alternatives. The goal is to **price to the value narrative** you’ve built.

---

## Practical Pricing Levers

- **Anchoring:** Show a higher reference (live ticket price, comparable PPVs) before revealing the PPV price.
- **Decoy tiers:** Use a slightly higher premium tier to make the core price feel more reasonable.
- **Framing:** Express price as “$X for a live, one-time experience” rather than “$X for a stream.”
- **Bundled value:** If premium includes replays, emphasize the total value (live + replay = two experiences).

---

## When to Avoid Pricing Tricks

PPV buyers are often skeptical about access quality. Overly clever pricing can undermine trust. Avoid:
- Fake discounts with unclear original pricing
- Flash “price drops” that repeat every week
- Overly complex add-on fees at checkout

The buyer should understand the total cost in under 10 seconds.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Price in PPV signals quality and legitimacy, not just affordability
- Anchoring and framing are effective when tied to real comparisons
- Simple, transparent pricing reduces refunds and chargebacks
- Premium tier design can elevate core conversion if value is distinct

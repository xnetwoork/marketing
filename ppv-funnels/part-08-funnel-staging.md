# Part 08: Funnel Staging

## Learning Objectives
- Define the stages of a PPV sales funnel from awareness to retention
- Assign the right metrics and content to each stage
- Prevent stage leakage by aligning messaging with buyer intent

---

## The PPV Funnel Is Time-Compressed

Unlike SaaS or subscription funnels, PPV funnels compress decision-making into hours or days. This means each stage must do one job well, without overlapping messages that confuse intent.

**Core stages:**
1. Awareness (teaser exposure)
2. Interest (landing engagement)
3. Consideration (proof + offer clarity)
4. Conversion (checkout)
5. Event-day activation (access and engagement)
6. Post-event retention (replays, next event)

---

## Stage Objectives and Signals

- **Awareness:** Maximize attention and curiosity. Signal: video view rate, CTR.
- **Interest:** Ensure clarity of the event and the outcome. Signal: time on page, scroll depth.
- **Consideration:** Reduce doubt with proof and detailed benefits. Signal: add-to-cart, FAQ clicks.
- **Conversion:** Remove friction and enforce trust. Signal: checkout completion.
- **Event-day activation:** Deliver access and reduce support load. Signal: login success, stream start.
- **Retention:** Offer replays and next-event pre-sale. Signal: replay usage, repeat purchase rate.

---

## Stage Alignment Rules

- Each stage should have one primary CTA.
- Don’t introduce new promises at checkout.
- Use the same event name and timing language everywhere.
- Post-event messaging should avoid repeating pre-event urgency.

Misalignment creates bounce and refund requests.

---

## Key Takeaways
- PPV funnels are time-compressed, so stage clarity is essential
- Each stage needs a single objective and a primary metric
- Consistent messaging across stages prevents trust erosion
- Event-day activation is a full funnel stage, not an afterthought

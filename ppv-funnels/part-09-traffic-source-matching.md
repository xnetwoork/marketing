# Part 09: Traffic Source Matching

## Learning Objectives
- Match traffic sources to PPV buyer intent and event timing
- Align channel expectations with landing page structure and offer framing
- Reduce wasted spend by using intent-aware distribution

---

## Channel-Intent Fit for PPV

Not all traffic is equal. PPV funnels depend on **intent alignment** between the channel and the buyer’s readiness. Sending low-intent traffic to a high-pressure checkout kills conversion and inflates costs.

**Intent tiers by channel:**
- **High intent:** Search ads, brand email list, partner referrals
- **Medium intent:** Retargeting, event preview content, influencer posts
- **Low intent:** Broad social, display placements, discovery feed ads

---

## Channel-Specific Landing Strategies

- **Search traffic:** Use clear pricing, event date, and direct CTA immediately.
- **Social discovery:** Lead with teaser and story, then introduce pricing.
- **Email list:** Emphasize loyalty, early access, or bundled value.
- **Affiliate traffic:** Provide tailored landing pages that match partner messaging.

Your landing page should mirror the promise and tone of the source channel.

---

## Timing the Channel Mix

- **Early stage (2–6 weeks out):** Focus on social, content, and creator amplification.
- **Mid stage (1–2 weeks out):** Shift to retargeting, email, and higher intent paid.
- **Final 48 hours:** Maximize search, direct-response ads, and SMS reminders.

PPV funnels are time-sensitive; channel allocation should change weekly.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Traffic source intent must match the funnel stage and landing experience
- Channel-specific landing adjustments improve conversion without extra spend
- Channel mix should shift as the event approaches
- Mismatched source messaging is a major cause of bounce and refunds

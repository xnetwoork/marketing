# Part 10: Landing Page Structure

## Learning Objectives
- Design a PPV landing page that converts across mobile, desktop, and TV-driven traffic
- Sequence content blocks to match buyer intent and reduce friction
- Use structural patterns that increase trust and urgency without clutter

---

## The PPV Landing Page Stack

A high-converting PPV page is not long by accident; it is long **by sequence**. Each section answers a specific buyer question.

1. **Hero block:** entropy teaser + primary promise + CTA
2. **Event clarity block:** date, time, device support, and access steps
3. **Outcome proof block:** testimonials, clips, statistics
4. **Offer block:** tiers and pricing with clear tradeoffs
5. **Risk reversal block:** refunds, replay, support
6. **FAQ block:** remove last objections
7. **Final CTA block:** clean restatement and purchase CTA

---

## Mobile-First Considerations

Most PPV traffic arrives on mobile, even if the stream is watched on TV. Prioritize:
- Sticky CTA or purchase button
- Short paragraphs and high-contrast sections
- Embedded trailer that does not auto-play with sound
- One-screen view of the core offer

If the CTA is not visible in the first 6 seconds, mobile drop-off spikes.

---

## Trust and Access Clarity

PPV buyers are anxious about access. Include:
- Device compatibility list
- “How it works” steps (3 bullets)
- Support response guarantee
- Clear event start time with timezone conversion

Trust beats persuasion in the final decision.

---

## Key Takeaways
- A PPV landing page must answer buyer questions in a strict sequence
- Mobile-first design is mandatory because purchase happens on phones
- Access clarity and support visibility reduce refund and chargeback risk
- A clean, repeated CTA outperforms multiple competing CTAs

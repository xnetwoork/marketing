# Part 14: Email Nurture Cadence

## Learning Objectives
- Build an email sequence that converts PPV leads without fatigue
- Align each email to a single funnel objective
- Use timing and content balance to maximize open and click rates

---

## The PPV Email Rhythm

PPV funnels need a **short, high-intensity** email sequence. The goal is to maintain momentum while reinforcing clarity and urgency.

**Recommended cadence (7–10 days out):**
1. **Teaser recap** (immediate)
2. **Story + stakes** (1–2 days later)
3. **Proof + past highlights** (2–3 days later)
4. **Offer clarification + pricing** (3–4 days later)
5. **Urgency reminder** (24–48 hours before)
6. **Last call + access instructions** (day of event)

---

## Email Objectives by Stage

- **Early emails:** Build narrative and anticipation
- **Mid emails:** Resolve objections with proof and clarity
- **Final emails:** Focus on time and access certainty

Each email should answer one question: Why care? Why trust? Why now?

---

## Subject Line and CTA Guidelines

- Use event name and time to reduce confusion
- Keep the CTA consistent (“Get Access,” “Reserve Your Stream”)
- Avoid gimmicks that resemble spam during the final 24 hours

---

## Key Takeaways
- PPV email sequences must be short, focused, and aligned to timing
- Each email should map to a single funnel objective
- The final emails should be about clarity and access, not new features
- Consistency in CTA and event naming reduces last-minute hesitation

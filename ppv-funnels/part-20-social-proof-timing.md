# Part 20: Social Proof Timing

## Learning Objectives
- Time social proof to maximize its conversion impact
- Select proof formats that match PPV buyer behavior
- Avoid proof overload that reduces clarity

---

## Social Proof Is a Timing Game

Social proof works best when it resolves doubt at the exact moment of decision. Too early and it’s ignored; too late and the buyer has already left. Timing is the difference between “nice to have” and “conversion leverage.”

---

## The 3 Timing Windows

1. **Post-clarity, pre-offer**
   - After the event is understood, before pricing is revealed
2. **Immediately before checkout CTA**
   - Short testimonials or trust badges near the action
3. **In confirmation follow-up**
   - Reinforces satisfaction and reduces refunds

---

## Best Proof Formats for PPV

- Short video clips from past viewers
- One-sentence quote overlays on event highlights
- “Watched by X viewers” counters (when real)
- Press or partner logos

Long case studies are rarely consumed in PPV funnels.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Social proof must appear immediately before high-friction decisions
- PPV audiences prefer short, visual proof formats
- Proof overload creates clutter and weakens the core message
- Reinforcing proof post-purchase reduces refund risk

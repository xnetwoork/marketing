# Part 23: Event-Day Funnel

## Learning Objectives
- Treat event day as a dedicated funnel stage with its own objectives
- Reduce access friction and support load during the live window
- Convert last-minute buyers without harming experience quality

---

## Event Day Is a Separate Funnel

Most PPV funnels stop at purchase, but event day is when the customer evaluates the product. This stage determines retention, replays, and future event sales.

---

## The Event-Day Checklist

1. **Access confirmation** — send live links via email + SMS
2. **Last-minute conversion** — ads and reminders focused on “starts soon”
3. **Support visibility** — live chat or fast-response channel
4. **Stream readiness** — load testing and redundancy in place
5. **Experience reinforcement** — pre-show countdown or warm-up content

---

## Messaging Priorities

- Focus on access certainty, not new features
- Use concise, calm language to avoid panic
- Provide clear support paths for common issues (login, device compatibility)

---

## Key Takeaways
- Event day is the product moment; treat it as a funnel stage
- Access confirmation and support visibility are conversion and retention levers
- Last-minute sales depend on clarity and confidence
- A smooth event-day experience reduces refunds and future churn

# Part 29: Community Flywheel

## Learning Objectives
- Use community engagement to increase PPV conversion and retention
- Design simple community touchpoints that don’t overwhelm operations
- Turn live engagement into future demand

---

## Why Community Matters in PPV

PPV is often a shared experience. When viewers feel part of a community, perceived value rises and word-of-mouth grows. Community features also reduce the temptation to pirate, because the **social layer** cannot be replicated.

---

## Community Flywheel Elements

1. **Pre-event discussion** — short prompts or countdown discussions
2. **Live interaction** — moderated chat, reactions, or watch parties
3. **Post-event recap** — highlight sharing and feedback threads

Each element should be easy to participate in, not a heavy commitment.

---

## Operational Constraints

- Use moderation to prevent toxic moments during live events
- Keep participation optional to avoid excluding silent viewers
- Provide clear community rules before the event starts

---

## Key Takeaways
- Community engagement increases PPV perceived value and retention
- A simple three-touchpoint flywheel is usually enough
- Moderation protects the experience and brand reputation
- Community content becomes marketing assets for future funnels

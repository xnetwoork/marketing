# Part 31: Behavior-Based Offers

## Learning Objectives
- Use behavioral signals to tailor PPV offers without confusing buyers
- Identify high-intent behaviors that justify offer adjustments
- Balance personalization with simplicity at checkout

---

## Why Behavior-Based Offers Matter

Not all buyers behave the same. A visitor who watches a full trailer is more likely to buy premium access than one who bounces after 10 seconds. Behavior-based offers increase conversion by matching the offer to the level of intent.

---

## Key Behavioral Signals

- **Trailer completion** (high intent)
- **FAQ clicks** (objection-driven)
- **Pricing section dwell time** (value comparison)
- **Checkout abandon** (friction-driven)

Map each behavior to a specific response, such as surfacing a premium tier or highlighting the guarantee.

---

## Implementation Guidelines

- Keep offer adjustments subtle and reversible
- Avoid showing different prices to different users unless explicitly allowed
- Use behavior to emphasize benefits, not to hide alternatives

Clarity and trust must remain the priority.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Behavioral signals can guide offer emphasis and increase conversion
- Use behavior to highlight benefits, not to fragment pricing
- Behavior-based adjustments should be subtle and transparent
- Over-personalization can backfire if it feels inconsistent

# Part 35: Conversion Rate Benchmarks

## Learning Objectives
- Establish realistic conversion targets for PPV funnels
- Use benchmarks to identify where the funnel is underperforming
- Avoid misleading comparisons that distort optimization priorities

---

## Why Benchmarks Matter

Without baseline expectations, teams chase the wrong optimizations. PPV conversion rates vary widely by event type, audience size, and channel mix. The goal is not to match a global benchmark but to **improve against your own historical baseline**.

---

## Benchmark Ranges (Directional)

Use these as starting points, not fixed rules:
- **Landing page → checkout start:** 2–6%
- **Checkout start → purchase:** 50–80%
- **Email list conversion (from engaged list):** 3–10%
- **Retargeting conversion:** 1–4%

Track by device and channel to avoid false conclusions.

---

## How to Use Benchmarks Correctly

- Compare only similar event types and audiences
- Use small weekly improvements as the primary goal
- Focus on the largest drop-off first

Benchmarks are guideposts, not performance grades.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Benchmarks provide direction but must be contextualized
- Device and channel splits are essential for accurate comparisons
- Small conversion lifts in high-leverage stages drive the biggest revenue gains
- Use your own historical data as the primary benchmark source

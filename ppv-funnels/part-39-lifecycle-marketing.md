# Part 39: Lifecycle Marketing

## Learning Objectives
- Turn PPV buyers into repeat customers across multiple events
- Build lifecycle messaging that respects timing and interest decay
- Create loyalty mechanics that increase long-term LTV

---

## PPV Is Not One-and-Done

The most profitable PPV platforms treat each event as an acquisition opportunity for the next one. Lifecycle marketing bridges that gap with timing, segmentation, and relevance.

---

## Lifecycle Segments

- **First-time buyers** — focus on satisfaction and trust
- **Repeat buyers** — reward with early access or bundled pricing
- **Dormant buyers** — re-engage with high-impact highlights

Each segment needs different messaging and offers.

---

## Lifecycle Touchpoints

1. **Post-event recap** (24–48 hours after)
2. **Replay reminder** (if applicable)
3. **Next event teaser** (2–4 weeks after)
4. **Early-bird access** for loyal buyers

The cadence should align with the event schedule, not a generic monthly newsletter.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Lifecycle marketing turns PPV into a recurring revenue model
- Segmenting by buyer history increases relevance and repeat purchase rates
- Post-event recap and early-bird access are high-leverage touchpoints
- Lifecycle cadence must match the event calendar, not arbitrary timing

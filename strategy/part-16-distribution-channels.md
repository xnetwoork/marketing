# Part 16: Distribution Channels

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the strategic role of distribution in marketing
- Learn how to evaluate and choose the right channels for your product and audience
- Build a multi-channel distribution strategy that creates competitive advantage

---

## Distribution Is a Strategic Decision

Distribution — how your product reaches customers — is one of the most underappreciated strategic levers in marketing. Many businesses default to obvious channels without asking whether those channels are actually optimal.

Amazon didn't just sell books online — it reimagined book distribution. Airbnb didn't just offer accommodation — it distributed supply and demand in a way hotels couldn't match. Tesla sells directly instead of through dealerships. Costco requires membership instead of open retail.

Each of these is a deliberate distribution decision that created competitive advantage.

---

## Types of Distribution Channels

### Direct Channels
You sell directly to the end customer without intermediaries.

**Examples:**
- E-commerce website (direct-to-consumer)
- Company-owned retail stores (Apple Stores)
- Direct sales team (B2B enterprise)
- Direct mail
- In-app purchases

**Advantages:** Control over customer experience, higher margins, direct customer relationship and data, ability to personalize

**Disadvantages:** Higher cost to build at scale, requires marketing investment to drive traffic

### Indirect Channels
You sell through intermediaries who reach customers on your behalf.

**Examples:**
- Retailers and distributors (CPG brands)
- Resellers and VARs (value-added resellers in B2B)
- Agents and brokers (insurance, real estate)
- Affiliates and publishers (performance marketing)
- Marketplaces (Amazon, App Store, Google Play, Etsy)

**Advantages:** Access to established audiences, lower upfront investment, faster distribution reach

**Disadvantages:** Loss of control, margin sharing, limited customer data, dependency on partner priorities

### Digital Distribution Channels

In the digital era, "place" (see [Part 12](./part-12-marketing-mix.md)) encompasses digital access points:

- **Search engines:** Organic (SEO) and paid (SEM) — customers searching for solutions find you
- **App stores:** iOS App Store, Google Play, Chrome Web Store, Salesforce AppExchange
- **Marketplaces:** Amazon, Etsy, Shopify App Store, G2, Capterra
- **Social platforms:** TikTok, Instagram, YouTube (both organic discovery and paid)
- **Partner integrations:** Native integrations with complementary products that expose you to their user base
- **API and developer ecosystem:** Building a platform that others extend (Stripe, Twilio)

## Channel Selection Framework

Evaluate potential channels on:

| Criterion | Questions |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Reach** | Does this channel reach enough of your target customers? |
| **Access** | Can you realistically get into this channel? What are the barriers? |
| **Cost** | What is the cost to acquire a customer through this channel? Is it sustainable? |
| **Control** | How much control do you retain over customer experience and data? |
| **Alignment** | Does this channel reinforce or conflict with your positioning? |
| **Scalability** | Can this channel scale as you grow, or will it hit a ceiling? |

---

## Channel Intensity Strategy

How widely should your product be distributed?

**Intensive distribution:** Available everywhere possible (FMCG like Coca-Cola, commodity software)
- Maximizes convenience and awareness
- Appropriate when purchase decisions are impulse-driven or low-consideration

**Selective distribution:** Available through chosen channels that fit your positioning
- Protects brand, improves partner commitment, manageable complexity
- Appropriate for most non-commodity products

**Exclusive distribution:** Available only through one channel or partner (luxury goods, high-touch B2B)
- Maximum control and margin, deepest partner commitment
- Appropriate when exclusivity reinforces premium positioning

## Go-To-Market Channel Mix

Most businesses benefit from a multi-channel approach, but must prioritize:

### Stage 1: Channel Discovery (Early-Stage)
Test multiple acquisition channels at low cost to find which ones produce the best customers (highest LTV, lowest CAC). Don't invest heavily until you've found signal.

### Stage 2: Channel Optimization (Growth Stage)
Double down on 2-3 highest-performing channels. Build systems, teams, and content specifically for those channels.

### Stage 3: Channel Diversification (Scale Stage)
Reduce single-channel dependency. If one channel accounts for 80%+ of your business, you're vulnerable. Add secondary channels to create resilience.

## Partner Channels in B2B

In B2B, partner channels deserve special attention:

**Resellers:** Third parties who buy your product and resell it
**VARs (Value-Added Resellers):** Add services, customization, or implementation on top of your product
**Systems Integrators (SIs):** Integrate your product into larger technology solutions
**Referral Partners:** Refer customers and earn a commission without handling the sale
**Technology Partners:** Complementary products that co-market, integrate, or bundle with yours

**Building a partner channel:**
1. Define ideal partner profile (who has complementary customer relationships?)
2. Create partner program with clear economics, enablement, and support
3. Invest in partner marketing and joint go-to-market programs
4. Track partner-sourced pipeline and revenue separately

## Distribution and Brand Alignment

Distribution decisions affect brand perception. Premium brands know that where you sell says as much about your brand as how you sell:

- A luxury watch brand in a bargain outlet weakens the premium position
- A security software company selling exclusively through established enterprise resellers signals trust
- A DTC brand refusing to sell on Amazon controls the narrative and customer relationship

Ask: Does this distribution channel reinforce or undermine my positioning?

---

## Digital Platform as Distribution

Platform-based distribution creates unique dynamics:

**Advantages:**
- Access to massive existing audiences (App Store has 700M+ users)
- Built-in discovery mechanisms (marketplace search, recommendations)
- Trust transfer (customers already trust the platform)

**Disadvantages:**
- Platform dependency risk (algorithm or policy changes can devastate growth)
- Revenue sharing (App Store takes 15-30%)
- Limited customer data and relationship

The strategic question: use platforms for initial distribution, but build direct channels alongside them to reduce dependency.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Distribution is a strategic decision that shapes brand, margin, and competitive advantage
- Direct channels give control and margin; indirect channels give scale and reach
- Evaluate channels on reach, access, cost, control, alignment, and scalability
- Distribution intensity should match your positioning: exclusive, selective, or intensive
- Avoid over-dependence on any single channel; diversify as you scale
